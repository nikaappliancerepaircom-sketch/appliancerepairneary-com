Gas Stove Repair Calgary Calgary's Trusted Specialists

Calgary is one of the better Canadian markets for gas appliances abundant Alberta natural gas, well-developed ATCO Gas distribution, and a homeowner culture comfortable with gas cooking. That comfort has translated into a high concentration of gas ranges and gas cooktops in Calgary kitchens, particularly in newer subdivisions like Cranston, Mahogany, Walden, Auburn Bay, Legacy, Aspen Woods, and Springbank Hill, and in renovated character homes across Inglewood, Mission, Hillhurst, and Bridgeland. Gas stoves are wonderful to cook on instant heat response, visible flame for control, and even thermal distribution but they are more complex to service than electric units, and Calgary's specific gas conditions create a few signature failure patterns.

Our Calgary gas stove technicians are trained on every gas range and cooktop configuration: sealed burner gas ranges, open-burner pro-style ranges (Wolf, Viking, Thermador), gas-only cooktops (Bosch, Miele, KitchenAid), and dual-fuel ranges that pair a gas cooktop with an electric oven (very common in Calgary new builds). We arrive with the parts most often needed: ignition modules, spark electrodes, burner caps and heads, gas valves, regulator assemblies, and the brand-specific connection kits required by ATCO Gas inspection standards. We do not replace gas supply lines or perform work upstream of the appliance shut-off that requires a Class B gas fitter but everything from the appliance shut-off forward is our scope.

Common issues

Common Gas Stove Problems We Fix in Calgary

Below are the gas stove failures we see most often on Calgary service calls, with brief diagnostics for each. Our technicians arrive with the parts inventory required for these specific issues, so most calls are completed in a single visit.

Burner clicks continuously after lighting
Spark electrode shorted to ground (often by spilled liquid or food debris carbonized on the ceramic insulator), or moisture in the spark module. We dry, clean, and replace electrodes as needed.
Burner will not light at all (no spark)
Ignition module failed, wire connection from module to electrode corroded, or main control board fault. We test spark output and trace the wiring.
Burner sparks but flame will not stay lit
Flame sensor (thermocouple on older units, flame rectification probe on modern ones) is contaminated or the gas valve is not staying open. Common after the burner has run rich for an extended time.
Flame is yellow and sooty instead of blue
Air-gas mixture wrong usually a partially blocked burner port, or the air shutter has shifted out of position. Calgary altitude makes burners run slightly richer than design, so this is more common than at sea level.
Gas smell when burner is off
Gas leak turn off the appliance shut-off valve immediately and call us. Most often a deteriorated gas line connection, a worn valve seat, or a regulator failure. We pressure-test with manometer.
Knob hard to turn / knob feels gritty
Valve stem dry or damaged. We service or replace the valve and ensure the knob clicks cleanly through the click-to-light position.
Pilot light will not stay lit (older standing-pilot models)
Thermocouple weak, pilot orifice clogged, or pilot tube partially blocked. We clean and align the pilot, and replace the thermocouple.
One burner has high flame, others normal
Burner orifice (jet) is oversized for that position, or the regulator is supplying inconsistent pressure. Could also be a manifold leak. We measure pressure at each burner.
Stove makes a "whoosh" sound when burner ignites
Delayed ignition spark not firing fast enough, or gas accumulating before flame catches. This is a safety concern. We service the spark module and electrode gap.
Burner caps loose or rattling
Mostly cosmetic but can affect flame pattern. We re-seat the cap, verify the burner head alignment, and replace if warped.

Calgary-specific

Calgary Altitude, Natural Gas, and Why Your Burners Behave Differently Here

Calgary sits at roughly 1,045 metres above sea level high enough that gas combustion characteristics are noticeably different from sea-level design specifications. This is not just trivia; it directly affects how your gas stove performs and how long components last.

At Calgary altitude, atmospheric pressure is roughly 13.5% lower than at sea level. That means there is less oxygen per cubic foot of air drawn into the burner, so the flame runs slightly richer (less oxygen relative to fuel) than the burner was designed for. Manufacturers that sell into the Alberta market generally compensate by either supplying altitude-specific orifices or by tuning the air shutter wider, but this is not always done correctly at installation. We see Calgary gas stoves that have been running rich for years yellow flame tips, soot accumulation around burner ports, and elevated CO output that sets off the kitchen detector but never gets diagnosed.

The second Calgary-specific factor is natural gas pressure stability. ATCO Gas distribution maintains supply pressure at a regulated value, but the in-home pressure at your stove can dip when other appliances cycle on. A typical Calgary new build with high-efficiency furnace, water heater, and gas range can see momentary pressure dips during severe cold spells when all three appliances cycle on simultaneously. Modern stoves with electronic ignition handle this fine, but older standing-pilot units and budget electronic-ignition models can experience burner extinction events during these dips. We see this pattern consistently across older Calgary character homes in Inglewood, Bridgeland, and Hillhurst where gas ranges from the early 2000s are still in service.

The third factor is hardness in the gas itself not water hardness, but trace components in the natural gas distribution stream. Alberta natural gas contains small amounts of mercaptan (added for odour) and trace hydrocarbons that, over years of use, deposit a thin film on burner orifices and spark electrodes. This deposit slowly chokes burner ports and reduces spark intensity. We routinely service Calgary gas burner orifices every 4-5 years for cleaning, even when no specific complaint exists, because preventative cleaning extends component life and improves combustion efficiency.

Calgary gas stove maintenance that prevents the major failures: First, every six months, lift the burner caps and clean the ports with a thin wire or pin never a toothpick (which can break off and lodge). Second, wipe the spark electrodes with a dry cloth at the same interval; oil and food residue conducts at low voltages and reduces spark energy. Third, listen to the burner ignition pattern if you hear delayed ignition (a "whoosh" instead of a clean light), schedule service before it gets worse. Fourth, if you renovated your kitchen and reused an existing gas range from a previous home, verify altitude orifices were swapped if the previous installation was below 600 metres. Fifth, never let boil-over liquids dry on burner heads they carbonize and create permanent ignition issues. Sixth, if you smell gas at any time, even faintly, treat it as a real leak: shut off the appliance valve and call us. We carry electronic combustible gas detectors and can pressure-test the appliance to leak-localize within 30 minutes.

One more important note for Calgary households with high-end gas ranges (Wolf, Viking, Thermador, Bertazzoni, La Cornue): these brands use brass burner components that respond differently to Calgary's gas profile. We are trained on the brand-specific service procedures, and we can re-jet for altitude correctly when units are imported or moved to Calgary from lower elevations.
