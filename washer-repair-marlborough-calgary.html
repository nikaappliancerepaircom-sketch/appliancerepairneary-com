Frequently Asked Questions — Marlborough Washer Repair

My 25-year-old Whirlpool top-loader won't spin — is it worth repairing?

Yes. Whirlpool Direct Drive top-loaders from the 1990s are exceptionally serviceable. The most common cause of spin failure is a broken motor coupler — a $100–$180 repair that restores full function. These machines are well worth keeping if the tub, drum, and cabinet are in good condition.

What causes the Samsung 5E error code?

The 5E (or SE) error code indicates a drain failure. The most common cause is a blocked coin/debris filter, accessible from the front panel of the machine. We also check the drain pump motor and drain hose. Most 5E errors are resolved in under an hour, and the repair is among the least expensive washer fixes we perform.

Washer Care Guide for Marlborough Calgary Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Marlborough Calgary homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Marlborough Calgary homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Marlborough Calgary homes.

Common Washer Problems We Repair in Marlborough Calgary

Washing machines in Marlborough Calgary face a combination of regular wear and Calgary-specific challenges — hard water mineral buildup from the Bow River supply at 150–200 ppm, and Calgary's Chinook temperature swings that stress seals and hoses throughout the year. These are the washer faults our technicians see most often:

  • →Drum not spinning — a failed lid switch (top-loaders) or door latch (front-loaders) prevents the spin cycle from engaging. Common on HE washers over 5 years old in Marlborough Calgary homes.
  • →Excessive vibration or grinding during spin — worn drum bearings are the usual cause. A bearing caught early (grinding noise) costs $200–$380 to fix; left untreated, the drum shaft fails and costs significantly more.
  • →Water not draining after the cycle — a blocked pump filter or failed drain pump. Front-loaders are particularly prone to small-item blockages in Marlborough Calgary laundry rooms.
  • →Water leaking onto the floor — door boot seals on front-loaders and hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's hard water and temperature cycling accelerate seal deterioration.
  • →F21, E3, or UL error codes — these indicate specific component failures. We diagnose and resolve with a definitive repair, not a reset workaround.

Most Marlborough Calgary washer repairs are resolved in one visit. Call us at the first grinding noise — a bearing caught early saves you from a much more expensive drum shaft replacement later.
