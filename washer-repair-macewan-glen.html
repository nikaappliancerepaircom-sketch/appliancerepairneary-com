  • GE top-load transmission seal failure — the lower tub seal leaks around the agitator shaft after years of use, causing water to drip into the motor compartment
  • GE lid switch contact wear — the microswitch inside the lid assembly loses contact over time, stopping the spin cycle from activating

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you service Samsung front-loaders in MacEwan Glen? +
Yes. Samsung front-load washers are among the most commonly serviced machines in MacEwan Glen. We carry door boot seals, drain pump assemblies, and control boards for Samsung front-loaders on the van.
My GE top-loader is dripping underneath during the spin cycle — what's leaking? +
Drips from below a GE top-loader during spin are almost always caused by the lower tub seal around the agitator shaft. When this seal wears, water centrifuged during spin escapes into the motor compartment. Early repair prevents motor damage — we recommend not delaying once you notice the drip.
How do I prevent mold on my Samsung front-load door seal? +
Leave the door ajar after every cycle to allow moisture to evaporate. Run the drum clean cycle monthly. Wipe the door seal folds dry after loads with heavily soiled laundry. These three habits eliminate the conditions that cause boot seal mold in most MacEwan Glen laundry rooms.
Is a 35-year-old Whirlpool top-loader worth repairing? +
It depends on the specific fault. Drive belt and lid switch repairs on older Whirlpool machines are economical at $90–$145. Transmission or tub bearing replacements on a very old machine may not be cost-effective. We assess machine condition honestly during the diagnostic and let you decide.
