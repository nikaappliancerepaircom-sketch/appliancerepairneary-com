Washer Care Guide for Braeside Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Braeside homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Braeside homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Braeside homes.

Common Washer Problems We Repair in Braeside

Washing machines in Braeside face a combination of regular wear and Calgary-specific challenges — hard water mineral buildup from the Bow River supply at 150–200 ppm, and Calgary's Chinook temperature swings that stress seals and hoses throughout the year. These are the washer faults our technicians see most often:

  • →Drum not spinning — a failed lid switch (top-loaders) or door latch (front-loaders) prevents the spin cycle from engaging. Common on HE washers over 5 years old in Braeside homes.
  • →Excessive vibration or grinding during spin — worn drum bearings are the usual cause. A bearing caught early (grinding noise) costs $200–$380 to fix; left untreated, the drum shaft fails and costs significantly more.
  • →Water not draining after the cycle — a blocked pump filter or failed drain pump. Front-loaders are particularly prone to small-item blockages in Braeside laundry rooms.
  • →Water leaking onto the floor — door boot seals on front-loaders and hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's hard water and temperature cycling accelerate seal deterioration.
  • →F21, E3, or UL error codes — these indicate specific component failures. We diagnose and resolve with a definitive repair, not a reset workaround.

Most Braeside washer repairs are resolved in one visit. Call us at the first grinding noise — a bearing caught early saves you from a much more expensive drum shaft replacement later.

My LG front-loader is making a loud rumbling noise — what's causing it? +
Almost certainly drum bearing failure — the most common front-loader repair at 10–15 years. The bearing wears progressively; the sound typically starts as a low hum during spin and increases over several months. Bearing replacement at $170–$320 restores quiet operation. For an LG front-loader at $1,000–$1,400 replacement cost, this is a clear value repair.
My washer drain pump filter keeps clogging — is that normal? +
In active households with outdoor clothing and sports gear, yes — more than average. The drain pump filter catches debris that makes it past the drum: grass seeds, sand, small stones, coins, and fabric fibres. Cleaning the filter every 2–3 months prevents motor strain and premature pump failure. We show you how during the service visit.
Do you work on Bosch stacked units? +
Yes. Bosch stacked washer-dryer units are increasingly common in renovated Braeside homes. They require careful disassembly and specific tooling — we come prepared.
