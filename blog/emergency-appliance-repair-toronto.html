What Counts as an Appliance Emergency in Toronto?

Not every appliance failure is an emergency — a dryer that stops heating is inconvenient, but you can hang laundry for a day. These situations are genuine emergencies that justify a same-day urgent call:

  • Fridge stopped cooling — food safety risk begins within 4 hours. This is always a priority call.
  • Washer flooding — active water leak from a washer can cause floor or ceiling damage in multi-unit buildings.
  • Gas appliance smell — a gas oven or dryer with a gas smell is not an appliance repair emergency. Call Enbridge Gas Emergency (1-866-763-5427) first, then us.
  • Dishwasher actively leaking — standing water under a dishwasher needs immediate attention to prevent subfloor damage.
  • Freezer failure with valuable contents — medication, breast milk, bulk meat — a freezer failure is time-sensitive.

What to Do While Waiting for Emergency Appliance Repair in Toronto

For a Non-Cooling Fridge

Keep the door closed as much as possible. A full fridge maintains safe temperature for about 4 hours with the door closed; a half-full fridge for about 2 hours. Use coolers with ice for insulin, medication, or high-value perishables while waiting for the technician.

For an Active Washer Leak

Turn off the water supply valves behind the washer immediately. If you can't locate them, turn off the main water supply. Lay towels around the machine and move rugs and furniture away from the water path.

For a Leaking Dishwasher

Turn off the dishwasher and turn off the water supply valve under the kitchen sink. Do not run the machine again until the technician assesses the leak source.
