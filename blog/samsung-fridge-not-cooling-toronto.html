Why Do Samsung Fridges Stop Cooling?

Samsung refrigerators are among the most popular in Canadian homes, particularly the French door and Twin Cooling models. They are capable appliances but have a few recurring failure modes that Toronto technicians see regularly:

1. Evaporator Coil Ice Buildup (Most Common)

The most frequent cause of a Samsung fridge not cooling is ice buildup on the evaporator coils at the back of the freezer compartment. When the auto-defrost system fails — usually due to a faulty defrost heater, defrost thermostat, or defrost timer — ice accumulates until it blocks airflow completely. The fridge section warms up while the freezer may still feel cold.

You can temporarily fix this by unplugging the fridge for 24–48 hours with the doors open to allow manual defrost. If the fridge cools again but the problem returns within weeks, the defrost system needs a proper repair. Cost to fix: $150–$250.

2. Evaporator Fan Motor Failure

If you hear the compressor running but no cold air is circulating, the evaporator fan motor may have failed. This fan draws air over the cold coils and pushes it into the fridge and freezer sections. Without it, cooling stops even if the compressor is working. Replacement fan motors for Samsung models typically cost $80–$150 in parts, with total repair around $180–$280.

3. Control Board Fault

Samsung's electronic control boards manage the defrost cycle, temperature sensors, and fan operations. A failed board can cause partial or total cooling loss. Control board replacement in Toronto typically runs $250–$400 including parts and labour.

4. Sealed System Issues

If the compressor is not running or the refrigerant is low, the sealed system is involved. This is the most expensive repair: $350–$600+. For older Samsung fridges (7+ years), it may be more economical to replace the unit than to invest in a sealed system repair.

Act fast: If your Samsung fridge stopped cooling, remove perishables immediately and place them in a cooler with ice. Dairy, meat, and leftovers can become unsafe within 4 hours above 4°C.

How to Diagnose a Samsung Fridge Not Cooling

1

Check the Temperature Display

If the display shows a temperature but the fridge feels warm, the thermistor or control board may be giving a false reading. If the display is blank or showing an error code, note the code — it speeds up diagnosis.

2

Listen for the Compressor and Fan

Put your ear near the back of the fridge. A running compressor makes a low hum. If you hear the compressor but no fan noise from inside the freezer, the evaporator fan has likely failed.

3

Try a Forced Defrost Cycle

On most Samsung models, hold Power Freeze and Power Cool simultaneously for 8 seconds. If the unit enters defrost mode and cools again afterward, the auto-defrost system has failed and needs professional repair.
