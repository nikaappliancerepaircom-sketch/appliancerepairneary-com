If your LG front-load washing machine has started making a loud rumbling, grinding, or roaring noise during the spin cycle — a noise that gets progressively worse over weeks — you almost certainly have a drum bearing failure. This is the most common LG washer repair call in Calgary, and for good reason: LG's popular WM-series front loaders are well-designed machines, but the drum bearing has a documented weakness that's accelerated by Calgary's hard water. This guide covers how to confirm the diagnosis, what the repair involves, and whether it makes financial sense to fix or replace.

What Is the Drum Bearing and Why Does It Fail?

The drum bearing is a sealed ball bearing assembly mounted in the rear of the washer tub — it supports the rotating drum on the shaft connected to the motor. Under normal operating conditions it should last 8–12 years. In Calgary, this lifespan is frequently shortened to 5–8 years because hard water (150–200 mg/L mineral content) causes calcium and magnesium deposits to work past the bearing seal over time. Once the seal is compromised, the bearing runs dry and begins to fail rapidly.

The spider arm (the three-pronged bracket that connects the drum to the bearing shaft) often fails simultaneously with the bearing, particularly on LG models. This is important because replacing only the bearing without inspecting the spider arm can result in a second repair within months.

How to Diagnose LG Drum Bearing Failure at Home

Drum Bearing Diagnosis — Step by Step

  1. Open the washer door and reach inside to grab the drum firmly
  2. Try to move the drum side-to-side and up-and-down — it should feel tight with virtually no play
  3. If you feel movement (wobble) in the drum, the bearing is worn
  4. Listen during the spin cycle: a bearing failure sounds like grinding gravel or a rumbling roar that intensifies with spin speed
  5. Check under the washer for rust-coloured water streaks — these indicate bearing seal failure allowing water to reach the bearing
  6. Run a spin-only cycle with an empty drum and listen — noise that gets louder as RPM increases points strongly to the bearing

If the drum has obvious wobble and the noise is clearly speed-dependent, the diagnosis is almost certainly drum bearing failure. At this point, the washer should be repaired or replaced promptly — running a failing bearing allows the vibration to damage additional components (motor coupling, drum seals, tub welds) and increases total repair cost.

The Repair — What's Involved

LG drum bearing replacement is a significant repair — not because it's technically complex, but because it's labour-intensive. The repair process involves:

  1. Removing the front panel, door boot seal, and drum assembly
  2. Splitting the outer tub (the tub is typically press-fit or welded, requiring a tub splitter tool)
  3. Pressing out the old bearing and pressing in the new one
  4. Inspecting and replacing the spider arm if worn or cracked (common on LG)
  5. Reassembling the tub, drum, and front panel
  6. Running test cycles to confirm noise elimination and proper balance

This typically takes 3–4 hours in a Calgary home. Our technicians carry LG drum bearing kits (bearing + seal + spider arm) on their vehicles for the most common model families — allowing same-visit completion in many cases.

Preventing Drum Bearing Failure in Calgary

You can't prevent bearing wear entirely, but you can slow it down:

  • Never overload the washer — excess weight accelerates bearing stress on every spin cycle
  • Use HE detergent in correct quantities — excess suds cause the drum to work harder to drain
  • Run a monthly drum clean cycle with a washer cleaner tablet — this helps flush mineral deposits from the tub and boot seal area
  • Inspect the door boot seal annually for cracks — a cracked seal allows water to reach the bearing shaft area faster
