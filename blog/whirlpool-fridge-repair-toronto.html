Top Reasons a Whirlpool Fridge Stops Cooling

Dirty Condenser Coils

This is the most common and most overlooked cause of a Whirlpool fridge not cooling properly. Whirlpool bottom-freezer and side-by-side models have condenser coils at the back bottom of the unit. Over 1–2 years, dust, pet hair, and debris coat the coils, reducing their ability to dissipate heat. The compressor runs longer and harder, eventually causing the fridge section to warm up. A professional coil cleaning costs $80–$120 and often restores full cooling performance without any parts needed.

Evaporator Fan Motor Failure

The evaporator fan sits inside the freezer compartment and circulates cold air into both the freezer and fridge sections. When it fails, the freezer may still feel cold but the refrigerator section warms up noticeably. You may also hear no fan noise when the freezer door is opened. Replacement fan motors for Whirlpool models run $60–$120 in parts, with total repair cost $160–$260.

Defrost System Failure

Whirlpool's auto-defrost system uses a defrost heater and thermostat to periodically melt frost off the evaporator coils. When either component fails, frost accumulates until airflow is completely blocked. Symptoms include the fridge warming up gradually over several days. A defrost heater or thermostat replacement costs $150–$250 in Toronto.

Thermostat or Temperature Control Board

If your Whirlpool fridge runs constantly or barely runs at all, the temperature control thermostat or main control board may have failed. Board replacement on newer Whirlpool digital models runs $200–$380.

Why Is My Whirlpool Fridge Making Noise?

Unusual noises from a Whirlpool fridge can indicate several different problems:

  • Loud buzzing or humming: Often a failing condenser fan motor or evaporator fan motor with worn bearings
  • Clicking every few minutes: Classic sign of a compressor attempting to start and failing — overload relay may need replacement
  • Rattling from behind: The drain pan or compressor mounting vibrating — often a quick fix
  • Gurgling or dripping sounds: Normal refrigerant circulation sounds — not a problem unless accompanied by loss of cooling
  • Knocking: Worn evaporator fan blade hitting frost buildup — defrost system issue

Whirlpool Fridge Leaking Water — What Causes It?

Water pooling inside or under a Whirlpool fridge typically comes from one of three sources: a clogged defrost drain (the most common — ice or debris blocks the drain hole in the freezer floor), a faulty ice maker water line connection, or a cracked water filter housing. Clogged drain repairs are typically $100–$180 in Toronto.

Do not ignore water leaks: A Whirlpool fridge leaking under the unit can warp hardwood floors, soak into subfloors, and cause mould. If you see water, address it within 24 hours.
