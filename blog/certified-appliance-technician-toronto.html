What Certifications Do Ontario Appliance Techs Need?

Gas Appliances: TSSA Licence Required

In Ontario, any work on gas-fuelled appliances — gas stoves, gas dryers, gas ovens, and gas ranges — is regulated by the Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA). A technician working on a gas appliance must hold one of the following gas-fitting licences:

  • G3 licence: Authorises work on domestic gas appliances with up to 400,000 BTUs input, using gas at a pressure no greater than 14 inches water column. This covers most home gas appliances.
  • G2 licence: A higher classification that covers a broader range of commercial and industrial gas systems in addition to domestic.

Hiring an unlicensed technician to work on your gas appliances is illegal in Ontario and can void your home insurance if a gas incident occurs. Always ask to see the TSSA licence number before allowing work on gas appliances. You can verify licences on the TSSA website.

Electrical Appliances: College of Trades and Manufacturer Certifications

For electrical appliances (refrigerators, dishwashers, electric dryers, washers, electric ovens), Ontario's regulatory framework is less prescriptive. The appliance service technician trade falls under the Ontario College of Trades, though it is a non-compulsory trade meaning certification is not legally mandated to practice.

As a result, manufacturer-issued training certifications have become the de facto standard for verifying competence. Major brands like Samsung, Whirlpool (which also covers Maytag and KitchenAid), Bosch, LG, and GE run their own certification programs for service technicians. A tech who holds Samsung Pro Certification or Whirlpool factory training has demonstrated brand-specific knowledge that goes beyond general electronics.

What Insurance Should an Appliance Tech Carry?

Beyond certifications, any professional appliance repair company operating in Toronto should carry:

  • General liability insurance: Minimum $1 million coverage. This protects you if the technician damages your appliance, your flooring, or causes any injury while working in your home.
  • Errors and omissions (E&O): Covers situations where the repair advice or work was incorrect and caused further damage.

You are within your rights to ask for a Certificate of Insurance before work begins. A company that cannot or will not provide one should not be working in your home.

For gas work, you can verify a TSSA licence at tssa.org. Enter the technician's name or licence number to confirm it is active and in good standing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What certifications does an appliance repair technician need in Ontario?
For electrical appliances, technicians should have credentials from the Ontario College of Trades (domestic appliance service) or manufacturer certification programs. For gas appliances, a TSSA gas-fitting licence (G2 or G3) is legally required.
Is appliance repair a regulated trade in Ontario?
Gas appliance work is fully regulated by the TSSA in Ontario — only TSSA-licensed technicians may legally work on gas lines and gas appliances. Electrical appliance repair has a lighter regulatory framework, making manufacturer certifications the primary credentialing mechanism.
Should I ask to see a technician's credentials before letting them into my home?
Yes. Any legitimate appliance technician near Toronto will be happy to confirm their credentials and employer. Ask for the company name, their technician ID, and proof of insurance if you want to be thorough.
How do I verify that an appliance repair company is insured in Toronto?
Ask the company for their Certificate of Insurance. A legitimate business carries general liability insurance of at least $1 million. This protects you if the technician damages your appliance or causes injury in your home.
