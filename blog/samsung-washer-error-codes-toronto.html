Samsung Washer Error Codes: Complete Reference Guide for Toronto

Samsung front-load and top-load washers use alphanumeric error codes to communicate faults. Here's what each code means and whether it's a DIY fix or a call-a-tech situation:

4E / 4C — Water Supply Error

The washer is not receiving enough water. Check that both hot and cold water taps are fully open and that the inlet hoses aren't kinked. Also check the inlet filter screens at the back of the machine — these can clog with debris. If the screens are clear and water supply is confirmed, the water inlet valve has likely failed. Repair cost: $150–$240 CAD.

5E / SE — Drainage Error

The washer cannot drain. Start by cleaning the drain filter (accessed via the small panel at the front bottom of the machine). Clear any lint, coins, or debris. If the 5E code persists after cleaning, the drain pump motor has failed. Repair cost: $150–$240 CAD.

UE / U6 — Unbalanced Load

The drum detected an unbalanced load during the spin cycle. Open the door, redistribute the laundry more evenly, and restart the spin. If UE appears on every cycle regardless of load balance, the suspension rods (top-load) or shock absorbers (front-load) are worn. Repair cost: $160–$270 CAD.

DC — Unbalance During Spin

Similar to UE but specific to front-load models. On first occurrence, redistribute the load. Recurring DC codes point to worn shock absorbers. Repair cost: $160–$270 CAD.

Sud / SD — Excess Suds

Too much detergent or non-HE detergent was used. The cycle will pause until suds reduce. Switch to HE (High Efficiency) detergent and use the correct amount. If Sud appears even with HE detergent at correct dosage, the pressure switch or control board may be triggering a false positive. Repair cost: $140–$250 CAD.

E3 / 3E — Motor Error

The drive motor or motor control board has a fault. This typically requires component-level diagnosis. The motor hall sensor is a common failure point on Samsung units. Repair cost: $200–$340 CAD.

OE / 0E — Overflow

Water in the drum has exceeded the maximum level. This is caused by a faulty water inlet valve that doesn't close properly, or a failed pressure sensor. Repair cost: $150–$250 CAD.

What to Do When Your Samsung Washer Shows an Error Code Near Toronto

1

Note the Error Code

Write down the exact code displayed. If it disappears quickly, it may still be stored in the washer's diagnostics, which our technician can retrieve.

2

Try a Power Reset

Unplug the washer from the wall outlet for 2 full minutes, then plug it back in. Some error codes (especially UE, Sud) clear after a reset.

3

Check the Filter and Hoses

For 5E/4E codes specifically, check the drain filter and inlet screens before calling. These are owner-accessible checks that may resolve the issue.

4

Call Us If the Code Returns

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the 5E error code mean on a Samsung washer?
The 5E (or SE) code means the washer cannot drain. Check for a clogged drain filter (behind the small access panel at the front bottom) and clear any blockage. If the code persists, the drain pump has likely failed and needs replacement.
What does the UE error mean on a Samsung washer?
UE means unbalanced load. Stop the washer, redistribute the laundry, and restart the cycle. If UE appears repeatedly even with balanced loads, the suspension rods or shock absorbers may need replacement.
Can Samsung washer error codes be cleared by resetting the machine?
Some Samsung error codes clear after a power reset (unplug for 1–2 minutes). However, codes caused by mechanical failures will reappear until the underlying fault is repaired.
How much does Samsung washer repair cost near Toronto?
Samsung washer repairs in Toronto typically cost $150–$330 CAD including parts and labour, depending on the error code and component involved. Drain pump and control board repairs are at the higher end.
