What Are the Most Common Dryer Problems We Repair Near You?

Dryer failures tend to follow predictable patterns. After thousands of service calls across the GTA, here's what we see most frequently:

  • Dryer not heating: Failed heating element (electric) or igniter/gas valve (gas). This is the number-one dryer complaint. Repair cost: $150–$270 CAD.
  • Dryer not tumbling: Broken drum belt or seized idler pulley. Repair cost: $130–$210 CAD.
  • Dryer takes too long to dry: Often a partially blocked exhaust vent or failing thermal fuse. Repair cost: $100–$180 CAD.
  • Dryer making loud noise: Worn drum glides, felt seal, or drum bearing. Repair cost: $160–$280 CAD.
  • Dryer stops mid-cycle: Overheating due to blocked venting or failed thermostats. Repair cost: $130–$230 CAD.
  • Dryer won't start: Door switch, thermal fuse, or control board failure. Repair cost: $140–$290 CAD.

Gas vs. Electric Dryer Repair Near Toronto — What's the Difference?

Both gas and electric dryers share the same mechanical components — drum, belt, bearings, motor — but their heating systems differ significantly. Electric dryers use a resistance heating element, while gas dryers rely on a gas valve, igniter, and flame sensor assembly.

Gas dryer repairs require a technician who holds appropriate Ontario licensing to work on gas appliances. All our technicians who handle gas dryer calls carry the required certifications and follow TSSA safety guidelines. Gas dryer heating repairs typically run $170–$300 CAD, slightly more than electric equivalents due to part costs and safety protocols.
