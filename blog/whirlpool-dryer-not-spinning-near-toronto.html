Why Is My Whirlpool Dryer Running but Not Spinning?

Whirlpool dryers — including models under the Maytag and Kenmore brands which share Whirlpool platforms — use a long serpentine belt that wraps around the drum, over the idler pulley, and around the motor pulley. When this belt breaks, the motor runs freely, you may hear it humming or the start button activates, but the drum sits completely still.

This is one of the most common appliance calls we receive across Toronto, Scarborough, North York, Etobicoke, Mississauga, Brampton, and surrounding GTA areas. The good news: it's also one of the most straightforward repairs, typically completed in under an hour.

The Three Most Common Causes of a Whirlpool Dryer Not Spinning

1. Broken Drum Belt

The drum drive belt (part number varies by model, commonly WPW10115436 or 661570V on older units) is a thin, ribbed belt about 92 inches long. Continuous heat cycling causes these belts to become brittle over time. When they snap, all drum rotation stops. Replacement cost: $130–$200 CAD including a new idler pulley.

2. Failed Idler Pulley

The idler pulley maintains tension on the drum belt. When the idler pulley bearing seizes, it creates a loud squealing noise before eventually causing the belt to jump off or break. If you heard a screeching noise in the days before the drum stopped spinning, a seized idler pulley is the likely culprit. Replacement cost: $120–$190 CAD.

3. Motor Failure or Thermal Overload

If the dryer makes absolutely no sound when started — no hum, no vibration — the motor may have failed or tripped its thermal overload protector. Thermal overloads trip when the dryer overheats, often due to a blocked exhaust vent. Motor-related repairs cost $180–$310 CAD and require more diagnosis time.

How to Diagnose Which Part Failed on Your Whirlpool Dryer

1

Listen When You Start the Dryer

Does the motor hum when you press start? If yes, the motor works — the belt is broken. No hum at all suggests motor or control board failure.

2

Try to Turn the Drum by Hand

Unplug the dryer. Open the door and try to spin the drum by hand. If it turns freely and easily (no resistance), the belt is broken. If it's stiff or grinds, check the idler pulley and drum bearings.

3

Check the Exhaust Vent

A clogged vent causes overheating, which trips thermal overloads and accelerates belt wear. Clear the vent from both ends before or alongside the repair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my Whirlpool dryer running but the drum not spinning?
If the motor runs but the drum doesn't turn, the most likely cause is a broken drum belt. The belt wraps around the drum and drives rotation. When it breaks, the motor runs freely but the drum stays still.
How much does Whirlpool dryer belt replacement cost near Toronto?
A Whirlpool dryer drum belt replacement in Toronto typically costs $130–$200 CAD including the belt, idler pulley inspection, and labour. We usually replace the belt and idler pulley together to prevent a repeat call.
Can a Whirlpool dryer drum not spinning be a motor failure?
Yes, but motor failure is less common than belt or idler pulley failure. If the dryer makes no sound at all when started, a motor failure or thermal overload is more likely than a broken belt.
