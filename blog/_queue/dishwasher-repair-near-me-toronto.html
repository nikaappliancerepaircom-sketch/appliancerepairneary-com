What Are the Most Common Dishwasher Problems Near Toronto?

Dishwasher problems range from minor inconveniences to complete wash failures. Across our years of service calls in Toronto, Scarborough, North York, Etobicoke, Mississauga, Brampton, Vaughan, Markham, and Richmond Hill, here are the failures we fix most often:

  • Dishwasher not draining: Clogged drain filter, blocked drain hose, or failed drain pump. Repair cost: $130–$220 CAD.
  • Dishwasher not cleaning dishes: Spray arm clogs, worn wash pump, or low water pressure. Repair cost: $140–$240 CAD.
  • Dishwasher leaking water: Door gasket wear, float switch failure, or pump seal crack. Repair cost: $130–$210 CAD.
  • Dishwasher not starting: Door latch, thermal fuse, or control panel failure. Repair cost: $140–$260 CAD.
  • Dishwasher leaving dishes wet: Heating element or rinse aid dispenser failure. Repair cost: $140–$230 CAD.
  • Dishwasher making loud noise: Wash motor bearing or broken spray arm bearing. Repair cost: $150–$270 CAD.

Dishwasher Repair vs. Replacement Near Toronto: What Makes Financial Sense?

A new dishwasher in Canada ranges from $650 to over $2,000 CAD depending on the brand. For any dishwasher under 8–10 years old, repair is almost always the better financial choice — especially when the repair costs less than $300 CAD. Factors that might tip toward replacement include: multiple simultaneous failures, discontinued parts (common for older European models), or a machine older than 12 years with chronic problems.

Our technicians will tell you honestly during the diagnostic if your dishwasher is worth repairing. We will never recommend a $400 repair on a $600 machine.
