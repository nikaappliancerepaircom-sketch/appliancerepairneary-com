Washer Repair Costs by Component — Toronto 2026

Drain Pump Replacement — $150–$240 CAD

One of the most common washer repairs across all brands. A failed drain pump prevents the machine from completing cycles and often leaves water in the drum. Labour is moderate — front-load drain pumps are easier to access than in some top-load configurations.

Door Boot Seal (Front-Load) — $160–$270 CAD

The rubber door gasket on front-load washers tears over time, causing water to leak onto the floor during cycles. Replacement requires removing the door and front panel and is more labour-intensive than most washer repairs. This is one of the more expensive routine repairs.

Lid Switch / Door Latch — $130–$200 CAD

Top-load washers use a lid switch that prevents spinning when the lid is open. When it fails, the washer won't spin at all. Front-load models have door latches that can fail similarly. These are quick repairs with affordable parts.

Water Inlet Valve — $150–$240 CAD

Controls hot and cold water entry into the drum. Failed inlet valves prevent filling or cause overfilling. Common across all brands.

Drum Bearing Replacement — $240–$380 CAD

One of the most expensive washer repairs. Drum bearings support the inner tub and when they fail, the washer makes a loud grinding or roaring noise. The outer drum often needs replacement on sealed-bearing designs, making this a significant job. Evaluate machine age before approving.

Drive Motor / Motor Coupling — $180–$320 CAD

Motor couplings (common on Whirlpool top-loaders) fail more often than full motors and are inexpensive. Full motor replacements are at the higher end of the range.

Control Board — $220–$370 CAD

Main control board failures manifest as erratic behaviour, error codes, or complete non-start. Boards for common brands are usually in-stock; some premium models may need ordering.

Shock Absorbers / Suspension Rods — $150–$260 CAD

Worn suspension causes excessive drum vibration, loud banging during spin, and UE/unbalanced errors. Usually replaced in pairs or sets of four.

Front-Load vs. Top-Load Washer Repair Costs in Toronto

Front-load washers generally cost more to repair because:

  • Parts (door boot seal, bearings, drum seals) are more expensive
  • Disassembly is more involved — more panels to remove, more time billed
  • Drum bearing replacements often require partial outer tub replacement on modern sealed designs

Expect front-load repairs to cost 15–25% more than comparable top-load repairs for the same fault type.

Should You Repair or Replace Your Washer in Toronto?

Apply the 50% rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of a replacement washer's price, replacement may make more financial sense. A mid-range front-load washer in Canada runs $900–$1,500 CAD in 2026. The threshold is roughly $450–$750 CAD for repair consideration. Most common washer faults fall well below this level.

Additional considerations: machine age (over 12 years, consider replacement), repeated failures over a short period, and availability of replacement parts for discontinued models.
