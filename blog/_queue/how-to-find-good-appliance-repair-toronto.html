Step 1: Check Certifications and Licensing

In Ontario, appliance repair technicians are regulated trades. For gas appliances (gas stoves, gas dryers, gas ovens), the technician must hold a gas-fitting licence from the Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA) — either a G2 (contractor-level) or G3 (domestic only) certification. Hiring an uncertified technician to work on gas appliances is illegal and dangerous.

For electrical appliances (fridges, dishwashers, electric dryers, etc.), technicians should hold credentials from the Ontario College of Trades or equivalent training from a recognized manufacturer program (like Whirlpool factory training or Samsung Pro certification). Ask to see credentials — a legitimate technician will show you without hesitation.

Step 2: Read Google Reviews Critically

Google reviews are the most useful signal when vetting a Toronto appliance repair company. Here is how to read them effectively:

  • Star rating baseline: Look for 4.7 stars or higher with at least 30–50 reviews. Below that sample size, ratings can be easily manipulated.
  • Review dates: A company with 200 reviews all posted in the same three-month window may have purchased reviews. Look for steady accumulation over time.
  • Specificity: Good reviews mention the technician's name, the appliance type, and what was fixed. Generic "great service" reviews with no detail are less reliable.
  • Response to negatives: See how the company responds to negative reviews. Defensiveness or blame-shifting is a red flag. Constructive responses show professionalism.

Step 3: Verify Transparent Pricing Practices

One of the most common complaints about Toronto appliance repair companies involves pricing surprises. Protect yourself by asking these questions before booking:

  • What is the diagnostic fee, and is it credited toward the repair?
  • Will I receive a written quote before you start work?
  • Are parts and labour both covered in the quoted price?
  • Is there an overtime or weekend surcharge?

Standard diagnostic fees in Toronto range from $80 to $100. If a company advertises a $35 diagnostic, they typically make up the difference by inflating part costs or adding unexplained labour charges. Conversely, if the diagnostic fee is $150 or more for a routine visit, that is also above market.

Red flag: Any technician who demands full payment before completing the repair, or who says they cannot provide a written quote, should be turned away. This is a common indicator of a scam operation.

Step 4: Ask About Warranty Coverage

A warranty tells you how much the company stands behind its work. In Toronto's appliance repair market, here is what to expect:

  • 30 days: Minimum acceptable coverage — shows the company will address problems that appear immediately after the repair
  • 90 days: Industry best practice — gives you time to confirm the fix held under normal use conditions
  • No warranty: Walk away. A company that offers no warranty has no accountability for its work

Step 5: Check Response Time and Availability

1

Same-Day vs. Next-Day Service

Good Toronto appliance repair companies offer same-day booking, especially for emergencies like a fridge not cooling with food inside. If the earliest a company can come is 5–7 days out, keep looking.

2

Local Dispatch

Companies that dispatch from within your neighbourhood arrive faster and charge less travel time. Ask where their technicians are based.

3

Parts Availability

Ask whether technicians carry common parts on their vans. Companies that need to order every part will ask you to wait days between the diagnostic visit and the actual repair.

What to Expect From a Reputable Appliance Repair Visit

A professional repair visit in Toronto should follow this sequence: the technician arrives within the booked window and calls 30 minutes ahead; they show their credentials if asked; they run a thorough diagnostic and document what they find; they present a written quote before touching any parts; they complete the repair (or explain clearly if a part needs to be ordered); they test the appliance before leaving; and they provide a receipt with the warranty terms printed on it.

If any of these steps are skipped — particularly the written quote or the post-repair test — that is worth noting. A company that skips the test run before leaving does not care whether your appliance actually works after they go.

Frequently Asked Questions

What certifications should an appliance repair company have in Toronto?
Look for technicians certified by the Ontario College of Trades or equivalent industry bodies. Gas appliance techs need a specific gas-fitting licence (G2 or G3) from the Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA).
How do I know if an appliance repair company in Toronto is trustworthy?
Check Google reviews (aim for 4.7+ stars with 50+ reviews), verify they offer written quotes before work starts, confirm they provide a warranty on parts and labour, and ask if they are insured.
What are red flags when hiring an appliance repair tech in Toronto?
Red flags include: quoting a price over the phone without seeing the appliance, demanding full payment upfront, no written quote or receipt, unusually low diagnostic fee ($30 or less), and no verifiable address or business registration.
How much should I expect to pay for appliance repair in Toronto?
A standard diagnostic visit costs $80–$100. Most repairs range from $120 to $400. If someone quotes below $50 for a diagnostic, treat that as a warning sign of bait-and-switch pricing.
