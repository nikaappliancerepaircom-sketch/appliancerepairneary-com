One of the most common questions we receive from Toronto homeowners is: "How long will the repair take?" It is a fair question. When your washer stops mid-cycle on laundry day or your fridge stops cooling on a warm evening, you need to know whether help is hours away or days away.

Our technicians have completed over 5,200 appliance repairs across Toronto and the GTA since 2017. Here is exactly what to expect at each stage of the repair process.

Stage 1: Response Time — How Long Until a Technician Arrives?

We dispatch to all Toronto and GTA locations within a 2 to 4 hour window from the time you book. For bookings made before noon on weekdays, same-morning arrival is often possible. Weekend slots are slightly more in demand but same-day service is available Monday through Sunday.

Tip: If your appliance situation is urgent (fridge not cooling with food inside, washer flooded), call rather than booking online. Phone bookings allow us to insert emergency calls into the same-day schedule more quickly.

Stage 2: Diagnosis — What Happens on Arrival?

Diagnosis takes 15 to 30 minutes for most appliances. Our technicians perform:

  • Visual inspection of the appliance and installation area
  • Error code reading (for digital appliances)
  • Component testing with diagnostic tools
  • Confirmation of the fault and written quote before any repair begins

You only pay the $119 diagnostic fee if you choose not to proceed with the repair. If you do proceed, the fee is waived and applied to the repair cost.

What Makes a Repair Take Longer?

  • Built-in or integrated appliances (panel removal adds 15–30 minutes)
  • High-rise condo access (elevator waiting, management coordination)
  • Older models requiring custom part sourcing
  • Dual-zone or multi-refrigeration systems (premium refrigerators)
  • Sealed system work (compressor, refrigerant) — always 2+ hours

When Parts Are Needed: Same-Day vs. Return Visit

Our service vans carry the most commonly needed parts for all major brands. In our experience across 5,200+ Toronto repairs, approximately:

  • 80% of repairs are completed on the first visit with parts from the van
  • 15% require a return visit with a specialty part ordered from our supplier (typically 1–3 business days)
  • 5% require longer part sourcing — usually premium brands (Miele, Sub-Zero, Viking) or discontinued models
Which parts are always on the van? Thermal fuses, door latches, drain pumps, water inlet valves, heating elements, belts, lid switches, and ice maker assemblies for Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, GE, Frigidaire, and Bosch. If you have one of these brands, the odds of same-day completion are very high.

How Long Does a Fridge Repair Take?

Refrigerator repair time depends on the fault:

  • Ice maker repair: 45–75 minutes (most common, parts usually on van)
  • Not cooling — evaporator fan: 45–60 minutes
  • Not cooling — defrost system: 60–90 minutes
  • Water leaking: 30–60 minutes
  • Compressor replacement: 2–3 hours (uncommon, usually advise cost vs. replace)

A fridge with food at risk is prioritized in our dispatch schedule. If you call before noon, we can usually have a technician to you the same morning.

How Long Does a Washer Repair Take?

Washer repairs average 45 to 75 minutes from first tool out to machine running:

  • Not draining (pump clog or filter): 20–40 minutes
  • Not draining (pump failure): 45–60 minutes
  • Not spinning (front-load, belt): 45–75 minutes
  • Not spinning (top-load, motor coupling): 30–50 minutes
  • Drum bearing replacement: 90–120 minutes

Condo Repairs: What Takes Longer in Toronto High-Rises?

Toronto has thousands of condos in Midtown, King West, The Waterfront, and Downtown. Condo repairs have additional time factors:

  • Elevator wait (3–15 minutes typically)
  • Building security clearance (1–5 minutes)
  • Narrow hallways requiring compact tool transport
  • Integrated panel-ready appliances (add 15–30 minutes for disassembly)

We contact building management ahead of time when needed and coordinate elevator booking to minimize wait times.
