What Are the Most Common Washing Machine Problems We Fix Near You?

Over thousands of service calls across Toronto, Scarborough, North York, Etobicoke, Mississauga, Brampton, Vaughan, Markham, and Richmond Hill, these are the washing machine faults we see most often:

  • Washer not draining: Usually caused by a clogged pump filter, a blocked drain hose, or a failed drain pump. Repair cost: $140–$220 CAD.
  • Washer not spinning: Door latch failures, lid switch issues, or worn drive belts are common culprits. Repair cost: $150–$260 CAD.
  • Washer leaking water: Door boot seal tears, worn hose clamps, or pump housing cracks. Repair cost: $130–$240 CAD.
  • Washer making loud noise: Drum bearing failure or foreign objects caught in the drum. Repair cost: $180–$320 CAD.
  • Washer not starting: Control board, thermal fuse, or power supply issues. Repair cost: $160–$290 CAD.
  • Error codes displayed: Samsung 4E, LG OE, Whirlpool F codes — our technicians diagnose and clear these with proper repairs, not just resets.

Is It Worth Repairing a Washing Machine, or Should I Replace It?

For most washers under 8–10 years old, repair is the smarter financial choice. A new mid-range front-load washer in Canada runs $900–$1,400 CAD, while the majority of repairs fall well below $350. Factors that tip the balance toward replacement include: repeated failures over short periods, refrigerant or sealed-system issues (not applicable to washers but worth noting), or machines older than 12 years where replacement parts are no longer manufactured.

Our technicians will give you an honest assessment during the diagnostic. We're not in the business of recommending unnecessary repairs or replacing parts that still have years of useful life.
