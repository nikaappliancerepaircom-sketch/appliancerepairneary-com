Common Bosch Washer Problems We Fix Near Toronto

Error Code E18 — Not Draining

E18 is the most common Bosch error code Toronto technicians encounter. It means the machine cannot complete its drain cycle within the expected time window. The most likely causes are a clogged pump filter (the small cap at the front bottom of the machine), a kinked or blocked drain hose, or a burned-out drain pump motor. The pump filter should be cleaned every 2–3 months — many homeowners never do this and it leads to this exact failure. Repair cost: $100–$200.

Not Spinning or Spinning Slowly

If clothes come out soaking wet after a full cycle, the spin cycle is not reaching full speed. Common culprits include an overloaded drum, a faulty door interlock switch (the machine will not spin if the electronic door lock is stuck in the open position), worn drum bearings (you'll hear a loud grinding noise during spin), or a motor control board fault. Bearing replacement on Bosch front-loaders requires significant disassembly and is one of the more labour-intensive repairs: $250–$400.

Leaking From the Door

The front door seal (bellow) on Bosch front-load washers can crack, collect debris, or develop tears, causing water to leak onto the floor during wash cycles. Replacing the door seal is straightforward but requires removing the front panel. Cost: $150–$250 including parts and labour.

Not Starting or Showing F21 / E12 Errors

These codes typically indicate a water intake issue — either the supply valve is faulty, the water inlet solenoid has failed, or there is insufficient water pressure at the machine. Check that the tap behind the washer is fully open before calling a technician.

Important: Never ignore water on the floor around a Bosch washer. A small door seal leak can cause significant floor and subfloor damage within days, especially in condos and townhomes with units below.
