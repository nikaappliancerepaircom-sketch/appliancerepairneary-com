Calgary has no shortage of appliance repair companies — a Google search returns dozens of results, many with identical-sounding names and generic websites. The challenge isn't finding someone who will show up; it's finding someone who will show up, diagnose accurately, quote honestly, repair correctly the first time, and stand behind their work. This guide gives you a systematic approach to vetting appliance repair companies in Calgary before you invite them into your home.

1. Look for Transparent, Upfront Pricing

A reliable appliance repair company in Calgary will tell you the diagnostic fee before they arrive. This fee — typically $55–$75 in Calgary's market — covers the technician's time to inspect the appliance and identify the fault. Reputable companies waive this fee when you proceed with the repair; less reputable operations charge it regardless and then quote inflated repair costs.

Warning signs at the pricing stage:

  • No diagnostic fee mentioned at all ("we only charge if we fix it") — this usually means a high minimum charge is buried elsewhere
  • A suspiciously low quote on the phone before anyone has seen the appliance
  • Refusing to give a fixed quote after the diagnosis — legitimate techs quote before they start work

2. Verify the Warranty on Repairs

Any Calgary appliance repair company worth hiring offers a written warranty on both parts and labour. The industry standard is 90 days; some companies offer 6 months or a year on certain parts. A company that won't commit to any warranty in writing is not confident in their own work — this is one of the clearest differentiators between professional and amateur operations.

Ask this question: "Do you provide a written warranty on parts and labour, and for how long?" If the answer is vague, non-committal, or "it depends on the part," look elsewhere.

3. Check Google Reviews — But Read Them Carefully

Google reviews are the most reliable signal for appliance repair companies in Calgary, but the total star rating alone isn't enough. Look at:

  • Volume: A company with 80+ reviews is more credible than one with 12, regardless of rating
  • Recency: Reviews from the last 6 months reflect current service quality, not two years ago
  • Specificity: Reviews that mention a specific appliance, technician, or neighbourhood are genuine; generic "great service!" reviews may not be
  • Response to negative reviews: How a company responds to complaints tells you more than the complaints themselves

Be cautious of companies with a large number of reviews and a perfect 5.0 — this can sometimes indicate review manipulation. A 4.6–4.8 with a large volume of specific, recent reviews is often a more reliable signal than a perfect score with fewer reviews.

4. Ask About Parts Sourcing

Calgary has several authorized parts distributors, and legitimate repair companies source from these rather than from generic overseas suppliers. For brands like Bosch, LG, Samsung, Whirlpool, and GE, OEM (original equipment manufacturer) parts matter — they fit correctly, work correctly, and don't void any remaining warranty coverage on the appliance.

Ask: "Do you use OEM parts or aftermarket?" A professional will use OEM for critical components (compressors, control boards, sealed system parts) and may use quality aftermarket for wear items like belts and door gaskets — and will be transparent about which is which.

5. Confirm Service Area Coverage

Many Calgary appliance repair companies advertise coverage of surrounding communities like Airdrie, Cochrane, Okotoks, and Chestermere, but some tack on large travel surcharges that aren't disclosed upfront. Confirm the total cost for your specific address before booking, particularly if you're in a suburban community. Our team covers all of these areas at no additional travel surcharge.

6. Red Flags — When to Hang Up

  • Technician asks to be paid cash only, upfront, before the repair is complete
  • No physical address or verifiable business registration in Alberta
  • Diagnosis fee is charged and then the "repair" is a vague "part cleaning" with no specific fault identified
  • Pressure to replace the appliance with a unit they happen to sell
  • Quote changes significantly after they've opened the appliance

7. Online Booking — A Modern Reliability Signal

Companies that offer transparent online booking are typically more organized, accountable, and customer-focused than those that operate purely via phone with no written record of the appointment. Online booking creates a paper trail, confirms the appointment details, and signals that the business is professionally operated.
