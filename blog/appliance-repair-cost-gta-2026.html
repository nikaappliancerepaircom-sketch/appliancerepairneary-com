What Affects the Price of Appliance Repair Near You

1. Brand and Parts Availability

Brand-name appliances from Samsung, LG, and Bosch often have higher parts costs than standard models from Whirlpool or GE, simply because their components are more complex and more expensive to manufacture. Parts for older or discontinued models can also be harder to source, increasing both the price and the lead time.

A well-stocked local technician near you in the GTA will often have common parts on their van, avoiding the need to order and wait. This is one of the hidden financial advantages of choosing a local provider over a big-box service centre.

2. Age of the Appliance

Older appliances (10+ years) present two challenges: parts may be harder to source and more expensive when available, and there is a higher chance that fixing one worn component will expose another failure soon after. This does not automatically mean you should replace — many 12-year-old fridges are worth repairing — but the tech should factor appliance age into the conversation with you.

3. Complexity of the Repair

A dryer belt or thermal fuse is a 30-minute job. A washer drum bearing replacement that requires partially disassembling the machine is 2–3 hours. Labour time is the biggest variable in appliance repair costs, so more complex repairs cost more regardless of brand or part price.

4. Whether Parts Are in Stock

If the part you need is available locally, repair can happen same-day or next-day. If it needs to be ordered, you may wait 3–7 business days, and expedited shipping can add $20–$60 to the cost. Asking upfront whether the technician carries your needed part on their van can save time and money.

Understanding the Diagnostic Fee

Most professional appliance repair companies in Toronto charge a diagnostic fee — typically $65–$90 — to cover travel to your address and the time to inspect and diagnose the appliance. This is fair and normal.

The critical thing to confirm is whether this fee is waived when you proceed with the repair. With our service, the answer is yes — always. The diagnostic fee is only charged if you decline the repair after seeing the quote. If you approve the work, it disappears from the invoice entirely.

Some companies do not waive it, meaning you pay the diagnostic fee on top of the repair cost. This is not necessarily shady, but it adds $65–$90 to your bill and should be disclosed before booking.

HST on Appliance Repair in Ontario

Yes, appliance repair services in Ontario are subject to HST at 13%. A repair quoted at $200 before tax will cost $226 after HST. This is not optional or avoidable — it is the law.

What you should watch for is whether a company quotes you pre- or post-tax. Reputable companies will quote before tax and state the HST separately on the invoice. If someone gives you a suspiciously round post-tax number without showing HST as a line item, ask for clarification.

The Repair vs. Replace Decision

A good technician will be honest with you about whether repair makes financial sense. A rough rule of thumb many industry professionals use: if the repair costs more than 50% of the replacement value of the appliance, replacement is worth considering — especially if the appliance is already 8–12 years old.

That said, this is not a hard rule. A $280 repair on a 6-year-old LG washer worth $800 new is absolutely worth doing. A $400 repair on a 14-year-old fridge that was worth $600 when new requires more thought.
