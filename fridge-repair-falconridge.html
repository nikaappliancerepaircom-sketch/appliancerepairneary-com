Fridge Repair in Falconridge — Reliable Service for Busy Households

Falconridge households rely heavily on their refrigerators — densely occupied homes with multi-generational families place a high demand on refrigerator capacity and cooling consistency. The neighbourhood was built in the 1970s and 1980s, and many original appliances have been replaced once or twice; the current generation of fridges is predominantly top-freezer and side-by-side models from Maytag, Kenmore, and GE, now reaching 15 to 25 years of service age. These units are approaching the phase where compressor stress, refrigerant line issues, and defrost system failures become increasingly common. We reach Falconridge in approximately 28 minutes from our 700 6th Ave SW office and can typically schedule same-day service for morning or afternoon windows.

The most frequent refrigerator repair call we receive from Falconridge involves the defrost system — specifically, defrost heater burnout or defrost thermostat failure causing frost accumulation behind the freezer panel. As ice builds up over weeks, it progressively blocks the evaporator fan, reducing airflow into the refrigerator compartment. The fridge section gradually warms while the freezer may appear normal or only slightly impacted. Homeowners often experience this as a "fridge not cooling" problem before the root cause — defrost cycle failure — becomes apparent. We disassemble the freezer panel, manually defrost the evaporator coil assembly, test the heater and thermostat components, and replace failed parts in a single visit for all common Maytag, Kenmore, and GE top-freezer models.

For older GE side-by-side refrigerators present in some Falconridge homes, the ice maker assembly and water inlet valve are common failure points after 15–20 years. Ice production stops, or the ice maker continuously cycles without producing ice — both symptoms that typically trace to the inlet valve solenoid or ice maker module. We carry common replacement modules for GE and Kenmore side-by-sides and can restore ice production same-day. Compressor failures on older units are assessed against replacement cost; for units under 20 years old with sound cabinet and refrigerant lines, compressor replacement can be economical.

Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Falconridge

Refrigerator repair calls in Falconridge peak in summer when compressors work harder, and in winter when Calgary's dramatic Chinook temperature swings — from −20°C to +15°C in 24 hours — stress door seals, defrost systems, and compressor components. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in Falconridge:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, evaporator fan, or thermostat failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in Falconridge.
  • →Ice building on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow. The fridge gradually warms up. More common in Falconridge during cold snaps.
  • →Water dispenser or ice maker not working — a failed water inlet valve or frozen fill line. Calgary's cold snaps can freeze water lines in poorly insulated spaces.
  • →Fridge running constantly — a worn door gasket lets cold air escape and forces the compressor to run non-stop. Calgary's Chinook cycles rapidly expand and contract rubber gaskets, accelerating deterioration.
  • →Clicking at startup or not starting — a failing start relay is a $60–$110 fix that prevents a full compressor replacement at $450–$750. Catching it early is critical.
