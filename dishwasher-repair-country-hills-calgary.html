Country Hills is one of north Calgary's largest residential communities, developed extensively through the 1990s and 2000s with a mix of laned homes, front-drive houses, and townhouses spread across a large footprint between Country Hills Boulevard and Stoney Trail. The dishwasher fleet in Country Hills homes reflects the 1990s-2000s construction era: GE, Whirlpool, and Samsung units are the most common brands, with machines now reaching 15 to 30 years of age and entering the period of first major component replacement.

Whirlpool dishwashers from the late 1990s and 2000s represent a large segment of Country Hills service calls. A common and distinctive Whirlpool failure in machines of this age is the pump and motor assembly lower seal — a rubber seal between the wash pump housing and the motor shaft that prevents water from the pump housing from entering the motor winding area. Over 20-plus years, this seal hardens and cracks, allowing water to seep past the motor shaft and enter the motor housing. The early symptom is a burning or electrical smell during the wash cycle, followed by intermittent wash motor failures as water contaminates the motor windings. We replace the seal if the contamination is in early stage, or the complete pump and motor assembly if winding contamination has progressed. Calgary's 150-200 ppm water hardness means this seal is subjected to mineral-bearing water throughout its service life — scale deposits at the seal junction accelerate the seal compression and cracking process compared to softer-water climates. GE dishwashers in Country Hills of similar vintage show spray arm rotation bearing seal wear — the seal around the main spray arm tower pivot allows water to bypass the spray circuit rather than building pressure through the spray arms, reducing cleaning effectiveness progressively.

Samsung dishwashers installed during Country Hills' 2000s construction build-out develop the sump filter and sensor failures that characterize Samsung units at this age. The turbidity sensor — a small optical sensor in the sump that measures how cloudy the wash water is to determine whether an additional rinse cycle is needed — accumulates scale deposits on its optical window from Calgary's hard water. When the sensor window is fouled, the machine cannot accurately read water clarity and defaults to extended cycle times or displays a cycle-related error code. We clean or replace the turbidity sensor and descale the sump area.

Dishwasher Maintenance Tips for Country Hills Calgary Homes

Calgary's Bow River water supply delivers 150–200 ppm of dissolved calcium and magnesium to Country Hills Calgary taps — well above the national average. Country Hills Calgary homeowners who follow a simple maintenance routine extend their dishwasher's life by 3–5 years compared to those who don't. Here is what our technicians recommend based on the repairs they see most often in Country Hills Calgary:

  • Monthly: Run an empty hot cycle with white vinegar or a citric-acid tablet (Finish or Cascade dishwasher cleaner). This dissolves mineral deposits in spray arms, the heating element, and the detergent dispenser — the most effective single step for Calgary hard water.
  • Every 2 months: Remove and rinse the filter basket under the tap. A clogged filter is the leading cause of draining failures and poor wash performance in Country Hills Calgary dishwashers. Takes 2 minutes.
  • Quarterly: Check the door gasket for cracks or hardening. Calgary's Chinook winds create dramatic temperature swings in winter — the door seal experiences thermal cycling that accelerates deterioration.
  • Always: Use rinse aid — in Calgary's hard water, rinse aid is not optional. It prevents scale spotting on glassware and helps the drying cycle perform correctly. Refill every 3–4 weeks.

If you notice any change in cleaning quality, draining, or unusual sounds from your Country Hills Calgary dishwasher, call us early. A $65 diagnostic caught before failure is far less expensive than a $280 pump replacement after it seizes.
