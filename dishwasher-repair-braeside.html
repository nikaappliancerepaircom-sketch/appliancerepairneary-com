Hard Water and Braeside Dishwashers

A Whirlpool or GE dishwasher that has been running Calgary tap water for 15 years has cycled through roughly 3,500–4,000 wash loads. At 150–200 ppm hardness, that's a significant mineral load accumulated in the spray arm holes, heating element surface, and inlet filter. The first sign is usually cleaning performance decline — dishes come out with white film or food residue even though the cycle completes. Left unaddressed, scale continues to accumulate and eventually the circulation pump impeller is affected, or the heating element fails from scale-induced thermal stress. We treat the delime service and spray arm replacement as the first intervention, and then assess whether the pump needs attention. Many Braeside dishwashers are restored to full performance without touching the pump.

Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Braeside
Poor Cleaning / White Film
Calgary hard water mineral deposits block spray arm jets and coat the heating element. Spray arm replacement and delime service restores cleaning performance.
Not Draining
Blocked drain pump or failed drain motor — standing water in the tub after the cycle ends. Same-day fix in most cases with parts on the van.
Leaking Water
Door gasket failure, spray arm housing crack, or inlet valve fault. Leaks onto hardwood or tile floors need fast attention — we prioritize these calls.
Door Latch / Won't Start
Plastic door latch mechanisms crack after 10+ years of use. Dishwasher won't start without a secure latch — fast, inexpensive fix.
Circulation Pump Failure
Wash pump or motor fails — dishes come out dirty, no wash spray heard during cycle. We carry Bosch and Whirlpool circulation pumps on the van.
Control Board / Error Codes
Unresponsive touch controls or mid-cycle stops. We diagnose the root cause — often a sensor or wiring issue rather than the board, saving significant cost.

Dishwasher Repair or Replace? A Guide for Braeside Homeowners

When your dishwasher breaks down in Braeside, the repair-vs-replace decision comes down to three factors: the age of the unit, the cost of the repair, and current replacement prices. Here is the framework our technicians use when advising Braeside customers:

The 50% Rule

If the repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost of a comparable new dishwasher, replacing usually makes more financial sense. A basic entry-level dishwasher in Calgary runs $550–$900 installed. If your repair quote is under $350, repairing is almost always the right decision.

Repair is the better choice when the unit is under 10 years old, the repair involves a single component (drain pump, door latch, heating element), and the brand is Bosch, KitchenAid, Miele, or Electrolux — brands with higher replacement costs and longer service lives.

Replacement may make more sense when the dishwasher is 12–15+ years old, you are facing multiple simultaneous failures, or the control board on a budget-brand unit costs $300+ to replace.

Our Braeside technicians give you an honest assessment during the diagnostic visit. We will tell you when replacement is the smarter financial decision rather than recommending an expensive repair on a unit near the end of its service life.
