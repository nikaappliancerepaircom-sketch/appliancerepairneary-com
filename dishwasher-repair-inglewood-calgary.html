Calgary Hard Water & Your Inglewood Dishwasher

Calgary's Bow River source water registers 150–200 ppm total dissolved solids — hard to very hard by Health Canada standards. In Inglewood, where many original homes pre-date water softener installations, this mineral load has been working on appliance components for generations. Spray arm nozzles calcify, heating elements develop insulating calcium coatings, and inlet valve screens restrict water flow. For homes with renovated kitchens and new European dishwashers, the same hard water still applies — Bosch and Miele units require correct rinse-aid dosing and periodic descaling cycles to maintain warranty-level performance. Our technicians carry citric-acid descaling tools, perform in-visit treatments where appropriate, and advise on the exact rinse-aid and salt settings for your specific model in Calgary's water conditions.

Local tip for Inglewood homeowners: If your property was recently renovated with a Bosch or Miele dishwasher, ensure the installer configured the internal water softener and rinse-aid dispenser for Calgary's 150–200 ppm water hardness.
