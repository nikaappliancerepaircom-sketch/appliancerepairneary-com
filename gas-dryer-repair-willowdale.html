Frequently Asked Questions

How does a gas dryer differ from an electric dryer?+

A gas dryer generates heat by burning natural gas through a burner assembly with an igniter, gas valve, solenoid coils, and flame sensor. Electric dryers use a resistive heating element instead. When a gas dryer runs without heating, the gas-specific ignition components are almost always responsible — not the drum, belt, or motor that both types share. Appliance Repair Near Me specializes in these gas-specific systems.

What is the gas dryer ignition sequence?+

The thermostat signals for heat, current flows to the igniter which heats to approximately 1,800 degrees Fahrenheit, the igniter's dropping resistance allows current through the solenoid coils which magnetically open the gas valve, gas flows and ignites, then the flame sensor confirms stable combustion. Each component has a distinct failure mode that Appliance Repair Near Me technicians diagnose in sequence.

Why does Appliance Repair Near Me inspect the dryer vent during every gas dryer call?+

A blocked exhaust vent is the leading cause of thermal fuse failure and a documented fire risk. Lint accumulation restricts airflow, causing the dryer to overheat and blow the safety fuse. Replacing the fuse without clearing the vent guarantees a repeat failure. Our technicians measure exhaust back-pressure and clean the vent whenever restriction exceeds the manufacturer limit.

Can a gas dryer produce dangerous carbon monoxide?+

Any gas combustion appliance can produce CO when burning fuel incompletely. A cracked gas dryer heat exchanger, an obstructed exhaust vent, or an improper gas connection can all increase CO output to hazardous levels. Install a carbon monoxide detector near your laundry area. Appliance Repair Near Me tests exhaust CO levels with a combustion analyzer after every gas dryer repair.
