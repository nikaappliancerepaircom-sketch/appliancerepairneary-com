Decision framework

Repair vs. Replace — The 50% Rule for Calgary Households

The standard appliance industry decision rule is the 50% rule: if the repair cost is more than 50% of the replacement cost, replace; if it's less than 50%, repair. We apply this rule honestly on every Calgary call and lay out the math before you decide.

When to repair

Repair is almost always the right call when: (1) the unit is under 10 years old and the fault is mechanical (pump, motor, element, switch); (2) the brand is premium (Bosch, Miele, Sub-Zero, Wolf, Thermador) where replacement cost runs $3,000+; (3) the fault is a one-time wear item (drain pump, ignitor, thermostat) on a fundamentally healthy unit; (4) the unit is a built-in (wall oven, OTR microwave, integrated dishwasher) where install cost on a replacement adds $400–$800 to the comparison.

When to replace

Replacement usually makes more sense when: (1) the unit is 15+ years old and a major component (compressor, transmission, sealed system) has failed; (2) the unit is a builder-grade microwave or budget dishwasher where new replacement runs $300–$500; (3) you've already had 2+ major repair calls on the same unit in 24 months; (4) the repair quote is over 50% of the replacement cost AND the unit is past its expected service life (10 years for laundry, 12–15 for refrigeration, 8–12 for dishwashers).

Calgary-specific factors

In Calgary, two factors push the math toward replacement: (1) high replacement-appliance availability at Calgary big-box and specialty retailers means new units are often installed within 48 hours, reducing the “wait time” cost of replacement; (2) Alberta sales tax structure (no PST) makes replacement appliances cheaper than equivalent purchases in BC, Saskatchewan, or eastern provinces. Both factors slightly favour replacement over repair in close-call cases.

Cost factors

Why Calgary Appliance Repair Prices Vary

Three primary factors drive Calgary appliance repair price variation across the ranges shown above. Understanding these helps you interpret a quote and recognize when a quote is fair vs. inflated.

Parts availability + sourcing

Mainstream brands (Whirlpool, Samsung, LG, GE, Frigidaire, Bosch, KitchenAid, Maytag) have strong parts availability through Alberta-based distributors — most parts arrive next-day or same-day. Premium and specialty brands (Sub-Zero, Wolf, Miele, Thermador, Viking) sometimes require parts sourced from out-of-province or US distributors with 3–5 day lead times. Vintage parts for 20+ year-old units sometimes require specialty NOS (new-old-stock) sourcing with 5–10 day lead times. We always quote parts lead time upfront.

Brand premium

Premium brand parts cost 30–80% more than equivalent mainstream parts (a Bosch dishwasher pump is typically $40–$60 more than a Whirlpool equivalent; a Sub-Zero condenser fan motor is $80–$120 more than a comparable GE motor). We don't mark up labour for premium brands — only the parts cost difference flows into the quote.

Urgency + scheduling

Standard scheduling is same-day when you book before noon Monday–Saturday, otherwise next-morning. Urgent same-day requests (cooling failures during summer heatwaves, holiday weekend laundry emergencies) sometimes carry a small priority surcharge ($40–$80) when we have to displace already-scheduled jobs. We always disclose this upfront before dispatching.
