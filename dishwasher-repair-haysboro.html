Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water is the dominant maintenance challenge for dishwashers in Haysboro. Hard-water minerals build up in spray arm jets and the filter basket over time, reducing water pressure through the wash arm and leaving dishes cloudy or food-spotted. On Frigidaire and GE models from the early 2000s, the circulation pump is particularly susceptible to mineral fouling — scale coats the impeller, reducing flow, and eventually the pump motor overheats. In many cases a thorough cleaning of the filter, spray arms, and pump inlet combined with a new spray arm restores full performance without replacing the pump. Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Haysboro

Calgary's water supply from the Bow River and Elbow River carries 150–200 ppm of dissolved minerals — significantly harder than the national average of 120 mg/L. Over time, this mineral load builds up in spray arms, heating elements, and detergent dispensers in Haysboro dishwashers, causing issues that appear mechanical but often start with water chemistry.

  • →Dishes coming out cloudy or dirty — mineral-blocked spray arms reduce water pressure to the nozzles. We clear the arms and run a full citric acid descaling service to restore wash performance.
  • →Water pooling at the bottom after the cycle — a clogged filter or failed drain pump. This is one of the most common dishwasher calls we receive from Haysboro homeowners.
  • →Door leaking onto kitchen floor — worn door gaskets or failed hinge springs. Gasket replacement in Haysboro typically runs $80–$140 all-in including labour.
  • →Dishes not drying properly — a failed heating element or vent fan. Calgary's hard water deposits on the element accelerate failure. Replacement: $150–$240 all-in.

My dishes come out spotty and cloudy — is this a hard water problem? +
Almost certainly. Calgary water at 150–200 ppm leaves calcium deposits on dishes and inside the dishwasher tub and spray arms. Using a rinse aid consistently and running a monthly cleaning cycle with a citric acid cleaner reduces spotting. If the spray arm jets are already blocked, we can replace the spray arm and do a thorough delime of the system — a $85–$140 service that restores cleaning performance significantly.
I'm a landlord — do you work on rental properties in Haysboro? +

My Frigidaire dishwasher from 2005 makes a grinding noise — should I repair it? +
Grinding is usually the wash pump impeller — either mineral-fouled or with a foreign object caught in it. In many cases, cleaning the pump inlet and replacing the spray arm resolves the noise. If the pump motor itself is failing, we assess whether a pump replacement at $150–$270 is sensible versus the cost of a new dishwasher. We'll give you an honest answer on-site.
