Hard Water and Palliser Dishwashers

A Whirlpool or Maytag dishwasher from 2008–2012 running Calgary tap water for 15+ years will have accumulated significant mineral deposits in its spray arm holes, inlet filter, and around the pump impeller. The first symptoms are poor cleaning — dishes come out with a gritty residue or food particles remain. As deposits worsen, the pump strains against reduced flow, heat builds up, and seal failure follows. In many cases a thorough delime service combined with a spray arm replacement and filter cleaning restores full performance without replacing the pump. We assess on-site and give you a clear picture of whether cleaning and a single part replacement will resolve the issue or whether the pump has been damaged beyond recovery.

Repair vs. Replace in Palliser

A 12–18 year old Whirlpool or Maytag is at the classic repair decision point. These machines were built to last 15–20 years under normal use; with one significant repair — typically pump motor, wash motor, or control board — they can deliver another five to eight years. At $120–$240 for most repairs versus $600–$1,000 for a comparable new machine plus installation, the math almost always favours repair. We bring honest assessments: if a machine has multiple concurrent faults or a failed pump on a high-kilometre chassis, we'll say so. But for a single fault on a 14-year-old Whirlpool, repair is almost always the right answer.

  • Whirlpool / Maytag 2005–2015: excellent repair candidates, parts widely available
  • GE 2008–2015: pump, control board, door latch — all serviceable
  • Bosch 2015–2020: circulation pump and control board — high repair value
  • Samsung 2015+: drain pump, door latch, water valve — repairable
Common Faults
Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Palliser
Not Cleaning Properly
Blocked spray arm jets from Calgary hard water mineral deposits, worn wash pump, or clogged filter. Very common in 10–18 year old Whirlpool and Maytag units.
Not Draining
Drain pump blockage, failed drain solenoid, or clogged drain hose. Standing water in the tub after a cycle — fixed same day in most cases.
Door Latch Failure
Dishwasher won't start or won't latch closed. Plastic latch mechanisms wear after 10+ years — a quick fix that restores normal operation.
Leaking Water
Failed door gasket, cracked spray arm, or faulty inlet valve. We identify the source and seal the leak to protect floors and cabinetry.
Noisy Operation
Worn wash pump bearing, debris in the chopper blade, or loose spray arm — all diagnosable on-site and repairable in most cases.
Control Board / Buttons
Unresponsive buttons, error codes, or cycles that stop mid-run. We test and repair or replace control boards for all major brands.

My dishwasher leaves dishes with white film — is that a hard water issue? +
Almost certainly. Calgary water at 150–200 ppm deposits calcium carbonate on dishes, the interior tub, and in the spray arm jets. A combination of dishwasher cleaning product (citric acid), jet unclogging, and potentially a new spray arm resolves most cases. If the heating element is coated, replacement may be needed for hot-water cleaning to return.
Is my 15-year-old Whirlpool dishwasher worth repairing? +
In most cases, yes — especially for a single fault. Whirlpool dishwashers from the 2008–2012 era are among the most durable built, and parts remain available and affordable. We'll diagnose on-site and give you a clear cost-vs-replace assessment before any work begins.
Can you repair a Maytag dishwasher? +
Yes. Maytag (now under Whirlpool) uses a parts platform shared across the Whirlpool family, so parts are readily available and inexpensive. Common Maytag repairs include pump, spray arm, door latch, and control board replacement.
