Lake Bonavista's mix of renovated and original homes creates a broad spectrum of refrigerator repair needs. Renovated kitchens in this affluent lake community frequently feature KitchenAid counter-depth or panel-ready units, and in some cases Sub-Zero built-in refrigerators — both of which require specialized repair knowledge and carry a significantly higher replacement cost than standard consumer fridges. These units are reaching the 20-to-25-year mark, and while compressor failure at that age is rarely worth addressing, defrost system faults, fan motor failures, and thermostat issues are extremely repairable and cost-effective at $150–$300. Common Fridge Repair Issues in Lake Bonavista

  • KitchenAid/Sub-Zero not cooling — sealed system or evaporator fan fault in premium units
  • GE/Kenmore defrost failure — ice buildup inside, warm fridge section
  • Ice maker not producing ice — water inlet valve or ice maker module fault
  • Water leaking inside cabinet — defrost drain blockage, common in cold climates
  • Fridge too warm or freezing food — thermostat or damper control fault
  • Loud compressor noise — compressor stress or condenser fan fault
