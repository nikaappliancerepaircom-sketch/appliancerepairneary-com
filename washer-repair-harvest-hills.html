Samsung front-load washers are the dominant service category in Harvest Hills. The most common Samsung front-loader failure at the 15-to-20-year mark is a worn or failed door latch reed switch. The reed switch is the sensor inside the door latch assembly that signals the control board when the door is securely closed. After many years of operation, the reed switch fails to make contact reliably when the door is latched, causing the machine to display a door-open fault code despite the door being physically closed. Homeowners sometimes apply extra force to the door or hold it shut as a workaround, but the correct repair is replacement of the door latch assembly with a new reed switch included. LG direct-drive front-loaders in Harvest Hills develop a stator assembly failure at a similar age — the direct-drive motor stator's winding insulation cracks after thermal cycling, causing the motor to fail intermittently or show an error code related to motor function. We test the stator winding resistance to distinguish between a failing stator and other motor issues before recommending the repair.

GE top-load washers in Harvest Hills homes show a high rate of water pump pulley bearing failure. The water pump in GE's top-load design of this era is driven from the motor via a belt and a pulley mounted on the pump shaft. The pump pulley bearing wears and develops a squealing noise during operation that progresses to a seized bearing and pump failure. Early intervention — replacing the bearing or the pump assembly before the bearing fully seizes — avoids the additional repair needed when a seized pump bearing damages the motor belt and motor pulley. We inspect and address bearing wear at the first symptom stage.

My Samsung washer shows a door open error but the door is definitely closed. +

My GE top-load washer has started making a squealing noise — what is it? +
Squealing in a GE top-load washer of this vintage typically comes from the water pump pulley bearing beginning to wear. We address this at the squealing stage — before the bearing seizes — which avoids additional repair to the belt and motor pulley that can occur when a seized pump bearing is allowed to run until full failure.

Washer Care Guide for Harvest Hills Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Harvest Hills homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Harvest Hills homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Harvest Hills homes.

Common Washer Problems We Repair in Harvest Hills

Washing machines in Harvest Hills face a combination of regular wear and Calgary-specific challenges — hard water mineral buildup from the Bow River supply at 150–200 ppm, and Calgary's Chinook temperature swings that stress seals and hoses throughout the year. These are the washer faults our technicians see most often:

  • →Drum not spinning — a failed lid switch (top-loaders) or door latch (front-loaders) prevents the spin cycle from engaging. Common on HE washers over 5 years old in Harvest Hills homes.
  • →Excessive vibration or grinding during spin — worn drum bearings are the usual cause. A bearing caught early (grinding noise) costs $200–$380 to fix; left untreated, the drum shaft fails and costs significantly more.
  • →Water not draining after the cycle — a blocked pump filter or failed drain pump. Front-loaders are particularly prone to small-item blockages in Harvest Hills laundry rooms.
  • →Water leaking onto the floor — door boot seals on front-loaders and hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's hard water and temperature cycling accelerate seal deterioration.
  • →F21, E3, or UL error codes — these indicate specific component failures. We diagnose and resolve with a definitive repair, not a reset workaround.

Most Harvest Hills washer repairs are resolved in one visit. Call us at the first grinding noise — a bearing caught early saves you from a much more expensive drum shaft replacement later.
