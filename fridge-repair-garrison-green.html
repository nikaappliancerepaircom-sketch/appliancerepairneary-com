Fridge Repair in Garrison Green — Counter-Depth and French Door Units in a Heritage Community

Garrison Green's kitchen layouts — typically found in townhomes and condos built between 2000 and 2012 — often feature counter-depth or French door refrigerators that fit flush against cabinetry. Samsung and LG French door units installed in these kitchens are now 13 to 25 years old, an age range when sealed system issues, evaporator fan motor failures, and ice maker malfunctions become frequent. Counter-depth units, while aesthetically suited to compact kitchens, also run their compressors harder due to reduced internal volume, which can accelerate wear on sealed system components over time.

The most common fridge fault we see in Garrison Green is frost accumulation in the freezer compartment, leading to the fresh food section gradually warming. This is a defrost system failure — typically a burned-out defrost heater, a failed defrost thermostat, or a defrost control board fault. The fix is straightforward and costs substantially less than a replacement refrigerator: defrost heater replacement in a Samsung or LG unit typically runs $130–$200 including labour. The second most common issue is ice maker failure, which in French door units often traces to a frozen water line, a faulty inlet valve, or a worn ice maker module. We carry replacement ice maker modules and inlet valves for Samsung and LG in stock.

Common Fridge Repair Issues in Garrison Green

  • Frost buildup in freezer — defrost heater, thermostat, or control board failure; fresh food section warms
  • Ice maker not working — frozen water line, faulty inlet valve, or worn ice maker module
  • Fridge not cooling — evaporator fan motor, compressor relay, or sealed system issue
  • Water dispenser fault — inlet valve, water filter bypass, or frozen line in door
  • Compressor clicking — start relay failure, common in LG and Samsung units in warm kitchens
  • Door seal deterioration — warm air infiltration causing ice buildup and compressor overwork
