Frequently Asked Questions — Royal Oak Dishwasher Repair

My Royal Oak dishwasher shows a 5C code — what does it mean?+
The Samsung 5C code (also displayed as 5E on some models) indicates a drain failure. The machine attempted to drain and the cycle timed out. First, clean the filter at the bottom of the dishwasher — a clogged filter is the most common cause and often a homeowner fix. If the filter is clean and the code persists, the drain pump motor has failed and needs replacement. We carry Samsung drain pump assemblies for same-day Royal Oak service.
Why are my dishes cloudy in Royal Oak?+
Cloudy dishes in Calgary are almost always caused by hard water mineral deposits. At 150 to 200 ppm, Calgary's Bow River water leaves a calcium carbonate film on glasses and dishes when rinse aid is insufficient or the dishwasher's internal softener (on Bosch models) is not calibrated for local water hardness. Recalibrating the Bosch softener, increasing rinse aid, and running a monthly citric acid cycle resolves the majority of Royal Oak cloudy dish complaints.
Is a 15-year-old dishwasher in Royal Oak worth repairing?+
Usually yes, if the repair is a pump, motor, or door latch issue. Dishwashers built between 2000 and 2010 in the mid-range Samsung, LG, and GE categories were reasonably well-built and are worth repairing if the repair cost is under 50% of a comparable new unit. If the control board has also failed on a 15-year-old machine, the economics become less clear — our $65 diagnostic walks you through the calculation.
