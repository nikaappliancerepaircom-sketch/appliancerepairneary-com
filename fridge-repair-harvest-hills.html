Samsung French-door refrigerators are the dominant call type in Harvest Hills. The Samsung French-door models from the 2005-2015 range develop a known issue with the ice maker evaporator cover — a plastic cover panel inside the upper fresh food section that develops cracks from the thermal stress of repeated freeze-thaw cycles in Calgary's climate. When the cover cracks, moist fresh food air infiltrates the ice maker compartment and accelerates ice accumulation around the ice maker mechanism. The ice buildup eventually jams the ice maker tray motor and causes the ice maker to fail, often accompanied by an error code. We inspect the cover, replace cracked panels, and address the accumulated ice buildup in a single visit. Samsung's sealed system issues — specifically a gradual refrigerant loss from the evaporator tubing — are also encountered in this neighbourhood's units and require a certified technician with refrigerant handling credentials to diagnose and repair correctly. GE side-by-side refrigerators in Harvest Hills show the thermistor failure pattern common in GE units of this era — a temperature sensor that provides incorrect readings to the control board, causing the compressor to cycle improperly and leading to temperature instability in one or both compartments. Thermistor replacement is a single-visit repair.

LG bottom-freezer refrigerators in Harvest Hills, purchased during the late 2000s and early 2010s, now present with defrost heater failures in their linear compressor systems. LG bottom-freezers use an electric defrost heater to melt frost from the evaporator coil; when the heater burns out or the defrost thermostat fails, frost accumulates over the evaporator until the coil is completely blocked, eliminating airflow to both compartments. The freezer temperature rises first, followed by the fresh food section, over a period of days. We replace the defrost heater and thermostat as a pair to avoid a repeat visit if only one component is replaced.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Harvest Hills Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Harvest Hills garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Harvest Hills garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Harvest Hills garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.

If your Harvest Hills fridge starts behaving oddly in October or November as Chinook season begins, call us for a diagnostic visit before it becomes a full failure during the coldest months.
