Dishwasher Repair in Cedarbrae — Older SW Calgary Neighbourhood Near Glenmore Trail

Cedarbrae is one of Calgary's established southwest communities, built predominantly through the 1970s and into the 1980s along the Glenmore Trail corridor. The neighbourhood is characterized by bungalows and split-level homes on generously sized lots — many of which have been owner-occupied for decades. The Frigidaire, GE, and Kenmore dishwashers installed in original kitchen renovations through the 1980s and 1990s are now 25 to 45 years old, operating well beyond their designed service lives. Many Cedarbrae homeowners have replaced original appliances at least once, and a second generation of Samsung and Whirlpool units from the early 2000s is also now reaching the age of common failure.

Calgary's hard water — at 150–200 ppm calcium and magnesium — has a compounding effect on dishwashers that have been running through decades of use. Spray arm nozzles accumulate mineral deposits year over year, progressively reducing water distribution to the upper and lower racks. Heating elements develop scale coatings that reduce their thermal efficiency. Water inlet valves develop calcium-restricted flow paths that slow the fill cycle and strain the wash pump. In Cedarbrae homes where a dishwasher has never been descaled, these effects are often visible the moment our technician opens the machine — thick white deposits on the heating element, spray arms with only one or two open nozzles, and an inlet valve screen that is nearly fully blocked. We descale as standard practice on any unit showing these symptoms, and it often makes a measurable difference in wash performance even before any parts are replaced.

Common Dishwasher Repair Issues in Cedarbrae

  • Severe hard water scale — decades of 150–200 ppm water deposit on spray arms, heating element, inlet valve
  • Drain pump failure — standing water after cycle, common in Frigidaire and GE units 20+ years old
  • Timer or control board fault — older electromechanical timer failure or newer board fault causing cycle issues
  • Door hinge and gasket deterioration — age-related seal failure causing water leakage onto floor
  • Heating element failure — dishes not drying, or water not reaching wash temperature
  • Water inlet valve blockage — scale-restricted valve preventing proper fill cycle
