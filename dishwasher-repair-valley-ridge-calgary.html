KitchenAid dishwashers are among the most prestigious brands we service in Valley Ridge. The most common KitchenAid failure at the 10 to 15 year mark is a failing upper wash arm support — the plastic housing that mounts the upper wash arm to the door-mounted rack degrades from repeated thermal cycling, eventually cracking and allowing the arm to drop and rotate ineffectively. The result is a dishwasher that cleans the lower rack thoroughly while leaving the upper rack consistently dirty. KitchenAid also uses a proprietary sump assembly that accumulates food debris and grease in homes where pre-rinsing is infrequent — the fine screen filter requires quarterly cleaning. Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water compounds this issue, adding calcium to the organic debris layer on the filter screen.

Samsung dishwashers in Valley Ridge are typically newer models installed in 2015–2022, and the most common early failure is the control panel touchpad — the capacitive touch surface develops dead zones from moisture ingress behind the panel, making certain cycles or temperature settings inaccessible. This is particularly frustrating on newer units that are otherwise fully functional. Control panel replacement restores full cycle access in a single visit. Bosch dishwashers in Valley Ridge occasionally develop door hinge cable failure — the plastic-coated cable that manages door opening tension frays and eventually snaps, causing the door to drop open suddenly.
