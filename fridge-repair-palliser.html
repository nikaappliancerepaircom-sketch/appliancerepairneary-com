Palliser's 1970s heritage creates an interesting refrigerator landscape. Some homes still run the original Frigidaire or GE that came with the house — machines now approaching 45–50 years that, improbably, still hold temperature. More commonly, the fridge in a Palliser kitchen is a mid-generation Whirlpool or Maytag side-by-side from the late 1990s or 2000s, or an LG French door model installed during a kitchen update in the past decade. Each generation presents distinct failure patterns, and our technicians carry the parts most likely to be needed for each.

The most common repair trigger in Palliser refrigerators is defrost system failure. In a 15–20 year old Whirlpool or Maytag, the defrost heater element burns out — the evaporator coil ices over, blocking airflow to the fresh food section, and the fridge warms while the freezer remains cold. Replacement of the defrost heater, thermal limiter, and defrost thermostat costs $140–$240 and is a straightforward same-day repair. For the LG French door generation (2012–2020), the characteristic fault is ice maker failure: the ice maker module fails, or the inlet valve screen blocks with Calgary hard water sediment, cutting off water flow to the ice maker assembly.

Garage Refrigerators in Calgary

Many Palliser homes have a second refrigerator in the attached garage — often the original kitchen fridge displaced during a renovation. Calgary winters create a specific problem for garage fridges: when ambient temperatures drop below 10°C, the compressor's pressure differential drops too low to trigger the cooling cycle, and the fridge stops cooling even though it runs continuously. This is not a mechanical fault — it's a thermostatic design issue. A garage kit (available for most Whirlpool, GE, and LG models) adds a heater strip near the thermostat that tricks the compressor into running at low ambient. We install garage kits and advise on placement to avoid this recurrence.

Refrigerator Problems We Fix in Palliser
Fridge Not Cooling
Defrost heater burnout or evaporator fan failure causing ice buildup that blocks airflow. Very common in 12–20 year old Whirlpool, Maytag, and GE models.
Ice Maker Not Working
Ice maker module failure, inlet valve blockage from hard water sediment, or ice bin auger jam. Common on LG, Samsung, and Whirlpool French door models.
Freezer Icing Over
Defrost system fault — heater, thermostat, or timer failure. Frost builds up on the evaporator coil and blocks the fan, warming the fridge compartment.
Water Leaking Inside
Blocked defrost drain causes meltwater to pool under the crisper drawers. A quick clear and flush restores proper drainage and prevents floor damage.
Compressor Clicking
Failed start relay or overload protector prevents the compressor from running. Often a $45–$90 part that restores full cooling without compressor replacement.
Worn Door Gasket
Cracked or deformed door seals let warm air in, forcing the compressor to work harder. Gasket replacement is a cost-effective repair on both old and newer fridges.

Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Palliser

Refrigerator repair calls in Palliser peak in summer when compressors work harder, and in winter when Calgary's dramatic Chinook temperature swings — from −20°C to +15°C in 24 hours — stress door seals, defrost systems, and compressor components. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in Palliser:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, evaporator fan, or thermostat failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in Palliser.
  • →Ice building on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow. The fridge gradually warms up. More common in Palliser during cold snaps.
  • →Water dispenser or ice maker not working — a failed water inlet valve or frozen fill line. Calgary's cold snaps can freeze water lines in poorly insulated spaces.
  • →Fridge running constantly — a worn door gasket lets cold air escape and forces the compressor to run non-stop. Calgary's Chinook cycles rapidly expand and contract rubber gaskets, accelerating deterioration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How soon can you come to Palliser? +
We're about 22 minutes from Palliser. For a warm fridge we prioritize same-day service to protect your food — book before noon for a same-day afternoon appointment in most cases.
My fridge is warm but the freezer is still cold — what's wrong? +
This is almost always a defrost system fault. The evaporator coil ices over and blocks the fan that circulates cold air to the fresh food section. The freezer stays cold because it's directly adjacent to the evaporator. Replacing the defrost heater, thermostat, and thermal limiter — about $140–$240 — resolves the issue in a single visit.
My garage fridge stopped working in winter — is it broken? +
Likely not — Calgary winters cause a known issue where garage fridges stop cooling when ambient temperatures fall below about 10°C. The compressor doesn't get a signal to run because the thermostat reads the ambient as already "cold enough." A garage heater kit resolves this. We can diagnose on-site and advise on the best solution for your specific model.
Is it worth repairing a 15-year-old Whirlpool refrigerator? +
Usually yes, for a single fault. A 15-year-old Whirlpool side-by-side has about 5–8 years of useful life remaining if repaired correctly. At $140–$280 for most repairs versus $1,200+ for a replacement, the economics favour repair unless you're seeing multiple simultaneous faults.
