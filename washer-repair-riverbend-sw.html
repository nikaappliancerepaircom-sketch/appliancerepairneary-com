Common Washer Repair Issues in Riverbend SW

  • Pump coupling failure — top-load washer won't agitate or spin, extremely common in older Whirlpool/GE machines
  • Lid switch fault — washer fills and drains but won't spin; lid switch bypasses spin cycle for safety
  • Samsung drain pump blockage — water in drum, error code displayed, pump filter or impeller clogged
  • Drum bearing failure — grinding sound during spin in front-load Samsung or LG units
  • Motor control board — front-load washer won't start, displays error, or stops mid-cycle
  • Door boot seal — water leaking at front of front-load washer, gasket torn or deteriorated

Frequently Asked Questions

My Whirlpool top-load washer fills with water but won't agitate or spin — what's the cause?+
This is the classic symptom of a worn pump coupling in a Whirlpool direct-drive top-loader. The coupling is a plastic part that connects the motor to the pump and transmission. When it wears out, the motor runs but neither agitates nor spins the drum. Replacement costs $90–$140 all-in and is typically completed in about 60 minutes. This is one of the most cost-effective appliance repairs available.
My Samsung front-load washer won't drain and shows an error code — what should I do?+
Don't run another cycle. A Samsung washer with a drainage error (typically 5C, OC, or 5E) has water in the drum that needs to be cleared before diagnosis. Our technician will drain the machine safely, check the pump filter for blockages, and assess the drain pump. In many cases the fix is a simple filter clean — if the pump is damaged, we replace it at $150–$220 all-in.

Washer Care Guide for Riverbend Sw Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Riverbend Sw homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Riverbend Sw homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Riverbend Sw homes.
