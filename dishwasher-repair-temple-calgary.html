Frigidaire dishwashers are a distinctive element of Temple's appliance profile. Frigidaire units of the 1990s and 2000s used a two-pump wash system — a separate circulation pump and drain pump — that, while effective when maintained, develops specific failure patterns under Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water conditions. The circulation pump seal deteriorates as calcium abrades the rubber contact surfaces, leading to water seeping under the dishwasher during the wash cycle. We identify and replace Frigidaire circulation pump seals on-site for Temple customers. The upper spray arm on these Frigidaire models relies on a rotating hub that receives water through a central post — calcium deposits on this post restrict rotational flow, causing the upper rack to wash poorly even when the lower rack performs normally.

Maytag dishwashers in Temple present a different challenge: the control board and timer assemblies on 1990s-era Maytag Jetclean models are increasingly difficult to source as OEM parts, but we carry compatible replacement boards and can typically resolve control failures within a single service visit. GE dishwashers in Temple, particularly the Nautilus and Profile-era models, develop float switch failures that cause machines to overfill or refuse to fill — both symptoms traceable to mineral deposits jamming the float switch mechanism. We service all three brands with equal competence and carry a broad parts inventory for Temple service calls.

Dishwasher Repair or Replace? A Guide for Temple Calgary Homeowners

When your dishwasher breaks down in Temple Calgary, the repair-vs-replace decision comes down to three factors: the age of the unit, the cost of the repair, and current replacement prices. Here is the framework our technicians use when advising Temple Calgary customers:

The 50% Rule

If the repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost of a comparable new dishwasher, replacing usually makes more financial sense. A basic entry-level dishwasher in Calgary runs $550–$900 installed. If your repair quote is under $350, repairing is almost always the right decision.

Repair is the better choice when the unit is under 10 years old, the repair involves a single component (drain pump, door latch, heating element), and the brand is Bosch, KitchenAid, Miele, or Electrolux — brands with higher replacement costs and longer service lives.

Replacement may make more sense when the dishwasher is 12–15+ years old, you are facing multiple simultaneous failures, or the control board on a budget-brand unit costs $300+ to replace.

Our Temple Calgary technicians give you an honest assessment during the diagnostic visit. We will tell you when replacement is the smarter financial decision rather than recommending an expensive repair on a unit near the end of its service life.
