At 5–15 years old, these machines are past their factory warranties and entering the high-maintenance phase of their lifecycle, where cooling system wear, ice maker component failures, and defrost system faults become increasingly common.

Samsung refrigerators from the 2010s installed in Redstone homes show a well-established pattern of ice maker control module degradation. The control module governs ice production cycles, defrost cycles within the ice maker compartment, and the ejector arm motor. When the module's relay contacts degrade, ice production becomes intermittent — producing ice some days and not others — before stopping entirely. Because the symptom can be caused by the module, the water inlet valve, the ejector motor, or the ice maker evaporator thermostat, correct diagnosis requires testing all four. We diagnose at the component level rather than immediately recommending a full ice maker assembly replacement, which is a more expensive repair than a targeted module or valve swap in most cases.

LG French-door and bottom-freezer refrigerators in Redstone face a defrost heater failure pattern typical of their generation. The defrost heater in these models is a glass-encased element that heats the evaporator coil to melt accumulated frost during each defrost cycle. As the glass sleeve ages with repeated thermal cycling — especially through Calgary winters where kitchen temperature differentials are greater — micro-cracks develop in the element and the heater eventually burns open. The result is progressive frost accumulation on the evaporator coil, reduced airflow, and eventual temperature rise in the fridge section while the freezer remains cold. We test heater continuity and thermostat calibration together before ordering parts, since both components fail independently and a complete defrost circuit test prevents a repeat service call if only one is replaced when both are failing.

Both Samsung and LG refrigerators in Redstone experience evaporator fan motor failures as they age past the 8–10-year mark. The evaporator fan — which circulates cold air from the evaporator coil through both the fridge and freezer compartments — operates continuously whenever the compressor runs. Over years of operation, fan motor bearings wear, initially producing a squealing or humming noise that grows louder, then eventually causing the motor to seize and stop. This produces the familiar warm-fridge-cold-freezer symptom pattern. We access the evaporator fan through the rear panel of the freezer compartment, replace the motor assembly, and test temperature recovery before completing the visit. Common Fridge Problems in Redstone

Samsung Ice Maker Control Module Degradation

Ice maker control module relay contact degradation causes intermittent ice production before complete failure. We test the module, inlet valve, ejector motor, and evaporator thermostat together at the component level, recommending targeted replacements rather than a full assembly swap where possible.

LG Defrost Heater Element Failure

LG's glass-encased defrost heater elements develop micro-cracks with repeated thermal cycling. When the element burns open, frost accumulates on the evaporator coil and fridge temperature rises while the freezer stays cold. We test heater continuity and defrost thermostat calibration together before ordering parts.

Evaporator Fan Motor Failure

Evaporator fan bearings in Samsung and LG refrigerators wear at the 8–10-year mark, initially producing escalating noise and then causing the motor to seize. When the fan stops, the fridge section warms while the freezer stays cold. We replace the motor assembly and test temperature recovery before completing the visit.

Water Dispenser & Ice Supply Line Issues

Dispenser dripping, slow flow, or no water typically indicates a failing water inlet valve or a supply line kink. In Calgary winters, supply tubes can partially freeze in door hinge zones where gasket integrity has declined. We inspect the full supply path from the inlet valve to the dispenser nozzle.

Condenser Fan & Coil Maintenance

Dust-blocked condenser coils and worn condenser fan motors cause compressors to overheat and cycle off prematurely. We clean condenser coils, test fan motor current draw, and replace fans where wear is evident — a maintenance-level repair that significantly extends compressor life in 10-year-old refrigerators.

Temperature Inconsistency & Thermistor Faults

Refrigerators that run too warm, too cold, or inconsistently between compartments often have failed thermistors or temperature control boards. We test thermistor resistance at known temperatures and compare to spec before replacing — a precise diagnostic that avoids misdiagnosis and repeat service calls.

Fridge Repair FAQ — Redstone

My Samsung fridge makes ice some days but not others. Why is it inconsistent?

Intermittent ice production in Samsung refrigerators is the early presentation of ice maker control module relay degradation. The relay makes intermittent contact as its surfaces degrade, producing ice when the contact is made and failing when it's not. This will progress to complete ice maker failure over weeks to months. We diagnose the module alongside the inlet valve and ejector motor to confirm the relay is the root cause before recommending replacement.

My LG fridge section is warm but the freezer is cold. What's the most likely cause?

This symptom pattern in LG refrigerators has two primary causes: evaporator fan motor failure or defrost heater failure with subsequent evaporator coil frost accumulation. We distinguish between them by checking for frost accumulation on the evaporator coil and testing fan motor operation. The appropriate repair differs significantly between the two — fan motor replacement is simpler and faster than a full defrost circuit service.

Should I repair or replace a Samsung or LG fridge that's 10–12 years old?

At 10–12 years, a refrigerator still has meaningful service life remaining if the compressor is functioning. Repair makes sense for ice maker components, defrost system parts, fan motors, and gaskets — individually these repairs are a fraction of replacement cost. We provide a transparent repair vs. replace analysis during the diagnostic visit, taking into account the specific repair cost, the machine's condition, and the current replacement market.
