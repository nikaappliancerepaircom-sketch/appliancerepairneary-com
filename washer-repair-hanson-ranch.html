Samsung front-loading washers in Hanson Ranch homes show a consistent pattern of drum bearing degradation. The rear drum bearing supports the drum shaft and absorbs the mechanical load of every spin cycle. As bearing grease depletes and the bearing races develop micro-pitting over years of operation, a characteristic rumbling or grinding noise emerges during the spin cycle — often described by homeowners as sounding like "rocks in the drum" even with a normal load. We assess bearing wear by hand-testing drum play and listening to the spin profile before recommending bearing replacement. For machines still in good structural condition, bearing replacement is a cost-effective repair with strong longevity outcomes.

LG washers with the direct-drive motor system — standard on most LG models sold in the mid-2000s onward — are prone to rotor position sensor (Hall sensor) failure as they age. The Hall sensor tells the motor controller the exact position of the rotor during operation. When it fails, the motor controller loses positional reference and the machine stops with a motor-related error code. This is often misdiagnosed as a stator or motor board failure, but the Hall sensor is an inexpensive component — and replacing it restores full operation in most cases without a more expensive stator swap.

GE washers from this era — both top-loading and the front-loading models GE began producing in the 2000s — frequently develop inlet valve solenoid failures. The dual-solenoid inlet valve controls hot and cold water entry independently. When one solenoid coil burns out, the machine fills only with one temperature of water or doesn't fill at all. We test solenoid resistance with a multimeter before ordering the part, confirming the valve as the fault rather than the water pressure or control board. Common Washer Problems in Hanson Ranch

Samsung Drum Bearing Degradation

Rear drum bearings in Samsung front-loaders develop grinding or rumbling noise during spin as grease depletes and races micro-pit. We assess bearing wear by hand-testing drum play, then replace the bearing assembly — a cost-effective repair that restores quiet operation with strong longevity outcomes.

LG Hall Sensor (Rotor Position) Failure

The Hall sensor monitors rotor position in LG's direct-drive motor system. Failure causes motor error codes and cycle stops — often misdiagnosed as a stator fault. We test the sensor independently before recommending a replacement, as the sensor is far less expensive than a full stator assembly.

GE Inlet Valve Solenoid Failure

When one or both inlet valve solenoid coils burn out, the washer fills with only one water temperature or doesn't fill at all. We test solenoid resistance before ordering parts, confirming the valve as the fault. A straightforward repair that resolves fill and cycle errors.

Front-Loader Door Boot Seal Tears

Door boot seals on Samsung and LG front-loaders develop cracks and tears after years of use. Water drips from the door glass or pools on the laundry floor. We replace boot seals with OEM parts and inspect the door latch mechanism for secondary wear in the same visit.

Control Board Error Codes

Modern washers accumulate control board faults that produce error codes corresponding to multiple possible failures. We trace the root cause — pump, valve, sensor, or motor — before recommending board replacement, ensuring you're not paying for a board swap when the actual fault is upstream.

Drain Pump Blockages & Failure

Washers that won't drain, finish incomplete cycles, or display drain error codes often have blocked or worn drain pumps. We clear blockages, inspect the impeller for damage, and replace the pump assembly where wear is confirmed — restoring full drain function in a single visit.

Washer Repair FAQ — Hanson Ranch

My LG washer stops with a motor error code. Does this mean I need a new stator?

Not necessarily. Motor error codes in LG direct-drive washers can be caused by the Hall sensor, rotor position sensor, motor board, or actual stator winding failure. We test each component in sequence. The Hall sensor is the most common single-point cause and is significantly less expensive to replace than the full stator assembly.

How loud is "too loud" for a washer during spin?

Normal spin noise is a steady hum. A grinding, rumbling, or metallic clanking noise during spin — particularly if it gets louder as spin speed increases — indicates drum bearing wear. We recommend addressing this promptly: worn bearings eventually cause drum shaft damage that makes the repair more extensive and expensive.

My GE washer takes a long time to fill. Is this a water pressure issue?

Slow fill can be caused by low water pressure, partially closed supply valves, or clogged inlet valve screens — common in Calgary homes where sediment builds up in supply lines. We check pressure, inspect valve screens, and test solenoid resistance. Most slow-fill issues are resolved with screen cleaning or an inlet valve replacement.

Do you carry parts for older Samsung and LG washer models on the truck?

We stock the most commonly replaced parts for Samsung and LG washers — inlet valves, door boot seals, Hall sensors, and drain pump assemblies — on our service vehicles. For less common parts, we typically source and schedule the return visit within 1–2 business days.
