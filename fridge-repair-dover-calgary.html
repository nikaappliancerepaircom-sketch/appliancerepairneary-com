Ice Maker Not Working

Frequently Asked Questions — Dover Fridge Repair

My GE fridge isn't cooling but the freezer works fine — what's the problem?

This is the classic symptom of a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat. Ice accumulates on the evaporator coil and blocks airflow to the fresh food section. We replace the defrost components on the spot — a $140–$220 repair that restores full function in one visit.

Is it worth fixing a Maytag refrigerator that's 20+ years old?

For most common repairs (defrost system, fan motors, ice maker), yes — these cost $130–$220 and add years of service to a machine that's otherwise sound. If the compressor or sealed system has failed on a 20-year-old unit, we'll give you an honest comparison before recommending expensive repairs on an ageing appliance.

Why is water pooling at the bottom of my refrigerator?

Water pooling in the fresh food section almost always means the defrost drain tube is blocked. As the evaporator coil defrost cycle runs, meltwater that should drain into the drip tray instead backs up and flows into the compartment. We flush the drain tube, clear any ice blockage, and check the drain heater to prevent recurrence.

Refrigerator repair calls in Dover Calgary peak in summer when compressors work harder, and in winter when Calgary's dramatic Chinook temperature swings — from −20°C to +15°C in 24 hours — stress door seals, defrost systems, and compressor components. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in Dover Calgary:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, evaporator fan, or thermostat failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in Dover Calgary.
  • →Ice building on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow. The fridge gradually warms up. More common in Dover Calgary during cold snaps.
