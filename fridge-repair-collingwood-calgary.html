Common Fridge Problems in Collingwood

  • GE top-mount defrost heater failure — the tubular calrod heater on older GE top-mount fridges fails open after years of thermal cycling, leading to evaporator frost accumulation and a rising fresh-food temperature
  • GE start relay click-no-cool — the small PTC start relay on the compressor fails, causing the familiar clicking sound every 4–5 minutes as the compressor fails to start
  • Kenmore bottom-mount evaporator fan motor bearing — the evaporator fan motor bearing seizes in older Kenmore bottom-freezer models, causing a squealing noise and warm fresh-food temperatures
  • Whirlpool ice maker water valve filter clog — sediment from Collingwood's older supply plumbing accumulates in the inlet valve mesh filter, reducing ice maker water flow to a trickle and causing small, incomplete ice cubes
  • LG French-door ice maker auger motor failure — the auger motor in the LG ice bin strip-outs its plastic gear set after 8–12 years of use, preventing crushed ice from dispensing while cubed ice continues to work
  • Samsung ice maker defrost sensor fault — the ice room defrost sensor drifts, causing excessive frost in the ice bin and auger jams that present as ice maker failure

Frequently Asked Questions

My 1970s GE fridge is clicking every few minutes and not cooling — is it the compressor? +
The clicking is almost certainly the PTC start relay, not the compressor itself. The relay is a small $20–$30 component that initiates the compressor start cycle. When it fails, the compressor cannot start and trips the overload every 4–5 minutes. Relay replacement costs $85–$130 and takes 20 minutes. The compressor itself in a 1970s GE is likely still in excellent condition.
Is it worth repairing a GE fridge that is nearly 50 years old? +
Very often yes, especially for defrost system and start relay faults. These machines were built with industrial-grade sealed systems designed to last 50+ years. As long as the compressor and refrigerant lines are intact, repair is almost always more cost-effective than a new appliance. We give you the full picture during the diagnostic.
My LG ice maker dispenses cubed ice but not crushed — what's the issue? +
When cubed ice works but crushed does not, the auger motor in the ice bin has failed — usually the plastic gear set strips out after 8–12 years of use. The ice is produced and stored normally, but the auger that grinds it for the crushed setting can no longer rotate. Auger motor replacement restores full crushed ice function.
Can you service Whirlpool side-by-side refrigerators in Collingwood? +
Yes. Whirlpool side-by-sides from the 1990s and 2000s are one of the most common fridge types in Collingwood's renovation-era kitchens. We carry parts for Whirlpool ice maker assemblies, inlet valves, defrost heaters, and fan motors on the service van.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Collingwood Calgary Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Collingwood Calgary garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Collingwood Calgary garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Collingwood Calgary garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.

If your Collingwood Calgary fridge starts behaving oddly in October or November as Chinook season begins, call us for a diagnostic visit before it becomes a full failure during the coldest months.
