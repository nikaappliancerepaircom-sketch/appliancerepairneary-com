Calgary Hard Water & Saddle Ridge Dishwashers

Calgary's Bow River municipal supply delivers 150–200 ppm total dissolved solids to every home in Saddle Ridge. This hardness level is classified as hard to very hard, and in a new community where builder-standard appliances were installed without water softeners, the impact on dishwasher components is significant. Spray arm nozzles — typically 1.5mm in diameter — accumulate calcium deposits that reduce their effective diameter to under 1mm within three to four years of regular use. Heating elements develop an insulating calcium carbonate coating that forces them to run hotter, consuming more energy before they fail. Our technicians carry citric-acid descaling tools and treat all water-contact components on every Saddle Ridge service call as standard practice.

Local tip for Saddle Ridge homeowners: Set your dishwasher's rinse-aid dispenser to level 4 or 5 (maximum) if you're on Calgary's municipal water supply. This is the single most effective way to prevent hard-water spotting and scale build-up in your machine — and it costs nothing extra compared to running low or empty.
