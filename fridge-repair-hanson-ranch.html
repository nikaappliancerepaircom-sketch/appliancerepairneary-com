Hanson Ranch's homes, built primarily through the 2000s, were equipped with builder-selected appliance packages that leaned heavily on Samsung French-door and side-by-side refrigerators, LG bottom-freezer models, and GE top-mount units. At 10–18 years old, these refrigerators are entering the phase where deferred maintenance and normal component wear converge to produce the most common repair scenarios we encounter in this community.

Samsung refrigerators from the 2000s are particularly prone to defrost system failures. The defrost system consists of three components — the defrost heater, defrost thermostat, and defrost timer or adaptive defrost control board. When any one of these fails, frost accumulates on the evaporator coil until airflow is restricted and the fridge section warms while the freezer remains cold. Hanson Ranch homeowners often describe this as "the fridge isn't keeping temperature but the freezer is fine" — the classic presentation of a blocked evaporator. We test the full defrost circuit, identify the failed component, and replace it in a single visit. This repair is significantly less expensive than compressor replacement and resolves the issue completely when properly diagnosed.

LG bottom-freezer refrigerators installed in Hanson Ranch face a documented challenge with the ice maker water supply tube in colder Calgary winters. The water supply tube that feeds the ice maker runs through the freezer door hinge area. When the door seal loses its integrity slightly with age, cold outside air creates a cold spot around the hinge where the supply tube can partially freeze. Ice production becomes intermittent — working some days, stopping others — until the freeze point becomes permanent. We address this by inspecting the supply tube routing, testing the door seal, and replacing the seal if it's contributing to the cold spot. If the tube has already sustained damage, we replace the tube section and reroute it away from the cold exposure zone.

GE top-mount and French-door refrigerators in this community frequently experience condenser fan motor failure as they age. The condenser fan draws air over the condenser coils at the back or bottom of the refrigerator to dissipate heat from the refrigeration cycle. When the fan motor's bearings wear, it initially squeals or hums at elevated volume, then stops entirely. A failed condenser fan causes the compressor to overheat and cycle off on thermal protection, producing intermittent or persistent temperature rise in both compartments. We access the fan, test motor current draw, and replace the motor where wear is confirmed — a straightforward repair that typically takes under an hour in the field.

Common Fridge Problems in Hanson Ranch

Samsung Defrost System Failure

Defrost heater burnout, thermostat failure, or adaptive defrost control fault causes evaporator coil ice buildup and fridge-section temperature rise. We test the full defrost circuit — heater continuity, thermostat calibration, control board output — and replace only the failed component for a cost-effective fix.

LG Ice Maker Supply Tube Freezing

Aging door seals allow cold air to create a freeze point around the supply tube hinge area. Ice production becomes intermittent. We inspect the tube routing, test the door seal, replace failing seals, and reroute the supply tube if it has sustained damage from repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

GE Condenser Fan Motor Wear

Condenser fan bearing wear produces escalating noise followed by complete failure. The compressor overheats and cycles off, causing temperature rise in both compartments. We test motor current draw and replace the fan motor assembly — a repair that typically completes in under an hour.

Evaporator Fan Motor Failure

When the evaporator fan stops, the fridge section warms while the freezer stays cold. Common in all three brands at the 10–15-year mark. We access the fan through the rear evaporator panel, replace the motor assembly, and test temperature recovery before completing the visit.

Temperature Control & Thermostat Issues

Refrigerators that run too cold or too warm may have a failed temperature control board, thermostat, or thermistor. We test the full temperature regulation circuit rather than assuming the thermostat is the culprit — incorrect diagnosis leads to repeat service calls.

Door Gasket Seal Replacement

Door gaskets on 10–15-year-old refrigerators lose magnetic grip and develop tears. Warm air infiltration forces the compressor to run excessive cycles and promotes frost formation near door seams. New gaskets restore the seal and reduce energy consumption and compressor stress.

Fridge Repair FAQ — Hanson Ranch

My Samsung fridge keeps food warm but the freezer is fine. What's wrong?

This is the classic presentation of an evaporator coil blocked with frost — caused by defrost system failure. The frozen coil restricts airflow into the fridge section while the freezer, which is adjacent to the coil, stays cold. We test the defrost heater, thermostat, and control board to identify the specific failed component and replace it in a single visit.

Why does my LG fridge make ice sometimes but not others?

Intermittent ice production in LG bottom-freezer models is often caused by a partially freezing water supply tube in the door hinge area. Aging door seals allow cold air infiltration that creates a freeze point. This condition worsens over time — addressing the door seal and supply tube routing early prevents complete ice maker failure.

How do I know if my fridge's compressor or fan has failed?

If both compartments are warm and the compressor isn't running, it's likely a compressor, start relay, or overload protector issue. If both compartments are warm but the compressor runs continuously, the condenser fan has probably failed, causing the compressor to overheat. A noisy hum followed by warmth in both compartments points to condenser fan failure. We diagnose both scenarios with measurement tools before recommending a repair path.
