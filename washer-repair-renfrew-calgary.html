Common Washer Issues in Renfrew Homes

  • Loud banging or grinding on spin — worn drum bearings in older Frigidaire and GE top-loaders
  • Not spinning — lid switch failure — common in top-load units over 15 years old
  • Not draining — drain pump failure or blocked pump filter
  • Agitator not working — agitator coupling or directional clutch failure in top-loaders
  • Front-load door seal leak — calcium-accelerated boot seal deterioration in newer units
  • Washer shaking excessively — suspension spring or shock absorber failure

Calgary Hard Water & Your Renfrew Washer

Renfrew's older top-load washers in non-softened homes face Calgary's 150–200 ppm water consistently — but the impact on top-loaders is less severe than on front-loaders, primarily manifesting as pump filter accumulation and detergent residue buildup in the drum. Front-loaders that have been introduced into Renfrew homes are more susceptible: the door boot seal in a front-loader accumulates mineral deposits between cycles, and without regular drum cleaning, degradation and musty odour follow within 2–3 years in Calgary's water conditions. Our technicians advise all Renfrew front-load washer clients on the monthly drum-clean cycle protocol appropriate for local water hardness, preventing recurring service calls for seal replacement.

Local tip for Renfrew homeowners: If your 1990s–2000s top-load washer is making a loud clunking noise only during the spin cycle, this is almost always worn drum bearings — not a transmission problem. Bearing replacement in a Kenmore or GE top-loader is typically far less expensive than transmission work and restores quiet operation reliably.
