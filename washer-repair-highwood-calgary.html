GE top-loaders in Highwood frequently present with motor coupler failures. The GE direct-drive motor coupler is a three-part assembly — two plastic drive forks connected by a rubber centre — that transmits motor torque to the transmission. After 25 years, the rubber centre hardens and cracks from repeated thermal cycling, producing a grinding noise during agitation that escalates into a complete no-agitate condition over a few months. We carry GE motor coupler kits on every north Calgary appointment and complete this repair in approximately 45 minutes. GE inlet valves in Highwood also develop mineral restriction from Calgary's 150-200 ppm water supply — fine calcium particles deposit on the valve screen over years, reducing fill rate until the machine generates unbalanced load errors because it cannot achieve the programmed water level within the allotted fill time.

Kenmore top-loaders in Highwood exhibit agitator dog wear — the small plastic pawls that enable the agitator's directional ratchet action. Worn dogs allow the agitator to turn freely in both directions, so clothes are swirled without the correct agitation stroke. This is a common repair on Kenmore machines with 15 or more years of service. LG direct-drive front-loaders in renovated Highwood laundry rooms occasionally develop drum bearing failure — the lower bearing that supports the drum shaft develops play after years of unbalanced load cycles. The characteristic symptom is a rhythmic rumble that increases in pitch and volume throughout the spin cycle. LG bearing replacement requires full drum disassembly, which we complete as a single-visit repair when parts are confirmed available.

Washer Repair in Highwood — FAQs

My GE washer makes a grinding noise then stops agitating — what's wrong? +
This is the signature of a failing motor coupler. The rubber centre of the three-part coupler cracks, allowing the drive forks to grind before disengaging completely. We stock GE motor couplers and typically complete this repair in under an hour in Highwood.
My Kenmore agitator spins freely in both directions — is that normal? +
No. A properly functioning agitator should ratchet — turning freely in one direction and locking in the other. Free rotation in both directions means the agitator dogs (directional pawls) are worn flat. Replacement restores correct agitation immediately.
