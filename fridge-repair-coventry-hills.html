Samsung French-door and side-by-side refrigerators from this period are particularly prone to ice maker evaporator cover cracking. The plastic housing over the ice maker evaporator coil undergoes repeated thermal cycling over years of operation, and hairline cracks develop that allow moist air to bypass the sealed compartment. Ice production slows or stops entirely, and frost accumulates in unusual places. In some cases the ice maker arm sensor also fails independently of the cover crack. We diagnose both components together and advise on the most efficient repair path — often a cover replacement resolves the frost ingress without requiring a full ice maker assembly swap.

LG bottom-freezer refrigerators installed in Coventry Hills homes face a well-documented challenge with their linear compressor systems. LG introduced the linear compressor as a quieter, more efficient alternative to conventional reciprocating compressors. While it succeeds on those metrics, the design concentrates mechanical stress differently, and units in the 10–18-year range sometimes develop compressor wear patterns that produce intermittent cooling loss — the fridge warms gradually while the compressor continues to run. We assess compressor performance with pressure testing and electrical load measurements before recommending a compressor swap or advising on replacement.

Whirlpool top-mount and French-door models from this era commonly experience evaporator fan motor failure. The evaporator fan circulates cold air from the evaporator coil into both the fridge and freezer compartments. When the fan motor seizes or its bearings wear, you hear an increase in noise followed by warm temperatures in the fridge section while the freezer remains cold. This is a straightforward repair — we access the fan through the rear evaporator panel and replace the motor assembly in a single visit. Common Fridge Problems in Coventry Hills

Samsung Ice Maker Evaporator Cover Cracking

Plastic evaporator covers in Samsung ice maker compartments crack after years of thermal cycling, allowing moist air ingress. Ice production slows or stops, and frost appears in unusual locations. We replace the cover and inspect the ice maker sensor and arm assembly in the same visit.

LG Linear Compressor Wear

LG's direct-drive linear compressor design concentrates mechanical stress differently than conventional compressors. In the 10–18-year range, wear produces intermittent cooling loss while the compressor continues running. We assess with pressure and electrical load testing before recommending repair or replacement.

Whirlpool Evaporator Fan Motor Failure

When the evaporator fan motor seizes or bearings wear, the fridge compartment warms while the freezer stays cold. Noise increases before complete failure. We access the fan through the rear evaporator panel and replace the motor assembly in a single visit.

Defrost System Failures

Defrost heater burnout, defrost thermostat failure, or defrost timer/control board faults cause ice buildup on the evaporator coil and eventual cooling loss. We test the full defrost circuit — heater continuity, thermostat calibration, timer function — and replace only failed components.

Condenser Coil Cleaning & Fan Service

Dust-blocked condenser coils and worn condenser fan motors cause compressors to overheat and cycle off prematurely, resulting in temperature drift. We clean coils, test fan motor current draw, and replace fans where wear is evident — important maintenance for fridges at the 15-year mark.

Door Gasket & Seal Replacement

Door gaskets on 20-year-old refrigerators often lose their magnetic grip or develop tears that allow warm air infiltration. This forces the compressor to run longer cycles and can cause frost buildup near the door seam. New gaskets restore the seal and reduce energy consumption.

Fridge Repair FAQ — Coventry Hills

My Samsung fridge stopped making ice but still keeps food cold. What's wrong?

Isolated ice maker failure with normal food cooling usually points to the ice maker unit itself — evaporator cover cracking, ice maker arm sensor failure, or ice maker control module fault. We inspect all three components and determine whether a targeted part replacement resolves the issue before recommending a full assembly swap.

How do I know if my LG compressor needs replacement versus repair?

LG linear compressor assessment requires pressure testing and electrical load measurement. A compressor running at correct pressures but with elevated current draw may have winding insulation breakdown. We provide an honest cost-benefit analysis — compressor replacement on a 15-year-old fridge may not be economical compared to a new unit, and we'll tell you so upfront.

The fridge section is warm but the freezer works fine. What causes this?

This pattern is classic evaporator fan motor failure. The fan circulates cold air from the evaporator coil (shared by both compartments) into the fridge section. When the fan stops, the freezer stays cold because it's adjacent to the coil, but cold air stops circulating into the fridge. A fan motor replacement typically resolves this completely.
