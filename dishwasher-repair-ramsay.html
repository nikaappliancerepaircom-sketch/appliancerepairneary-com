Common Dishwasher Issues in Ramsay Homes

  • Hard-water scale on spray arms and heating element — Calgary's 150–200 ppm water blocks nozzles and coats elements within 2–3 years
  • Not draining — standing water — drain pump failure, often accelerated by mineral deposits on the impeller
  • Door latch or gasket failure — heritage homes with 1990s–2000s dishwashers frequently need latch and seal replacement
  • Dishes not clean despite running — blocked spray arms or failed wash motor
  • Bosch E24 / E25 errors — post-renovation Bosch units with circulation pump or drain pump issues
  • Unusual noise during cycle — worn wash motor bearings or debris in pump assembly

Calgary Hard Water & Your Ramsay Dishwasher

Ramsay's proximity to the Elbow River is a local landmark, but it's the Bow River water supply running through Calgary mains at 150–200 ppm that most affects household appliances. In a Ramsay bungalow without a water softener — common in homes that haven't been renovated — the heating element, spray arms, and inlet valve screen are the first casualties of this mineral load. Our technicians carry citric-acid descaling solution and perform in-visit treatments as part of service calls when calcium buildup is detected. We also calibrate the rinse-aid dispenser to the dosing level appropriate for Calgary's water conditions, which significantly extends the interval between descaling interventions.

Local tip for Ramsay homeowners: If your heritage home's dishwasher is a late-1990s Kenmore or early-2000s Whirlpool and has never had the spray arms soaked in descaling solution, consider booking a service call even if the machine is technically running. Proactive descaling costs a fraction of a pump or heating element replacement.
