Whirlpool refrigerators in Ranchlands develop defrost thermostat and defrost heater failures as the primary age-related fault pattern. The defrost cycle runs twice daily to melt frost off the evaporator coil; when the thermostat opens prematurely from age-related contact wear, the heater does not run long enough to fully defrost the coil. Partial frost accumulation builds up over days until airflow is restricted, causing the fresh food compartment to warm progressively. Unlike a total defrost system failure, the freezer may remain adequately cold for some time, masking the problem. We run a full defrost cycle diagnosis on every Whirlpool call in Ranchlands, testing thermostat trip point, heater resistance, and timer motor function together. GE water inlet valve assemblies in Ranchlands develop a dual-valve failure pattern — the valve body supplies both the ice maker and the water dispenser through separate solenoids. Mineral scale from Calgary's 150-200 ppm water accumulates on the valve seat, causing restricted flow to the ice maker while the dispenser may remain functional, leading to a misdiagnosis of ice maker module failure rather than the actual valve restriction.

Samsung French-door refrigerators in renovated Ranchlands kitchens present ice maker freeze-over issues consistent with our Calgary-wide pattern — mineral-encrusted inlet valves dripping slowly after closing, with the drip freezing in the fill tube during the defrost off-cycle. We address the valve and fill tube together rather than clearing the tube alone, which prevents immediate recurrence. Door gasket seal replacement is also a common Ranchlands call across all brands — 40-plus-year-old gaskets in original-build Whirlpool and GE fridges have hardened beyond the point of maintaining an effective seal, allowing ambient air infiltration that causes the compressor to run continuously and the interior to fluctuate in temperature.

Fridge Repair in Ranchlands — FAQs

My Whirlpool fridge's food section is getting warm but the freezer seems okay — what's happening? +
This pattern in older Whirlpool fridges usually indicates a partial defrost failure — the defrost thermostat is tripping prematurely, preventing full coil defrost. Frost accumulates over several days until airflow is restricted. We run a complete defrost system diagnosis — thermostat, heater, and timer — to identify the specific fault.
My GE fridge's ice maker stopped working but the water dispenser still works — is the ice maker broken? +
Not necessarily. GE's dual inlet valve supplies both systems through separate solenoids. Mineral scale from Calgary's hard water often restricts the ice maker solenoid port while leaving the dispenser port clear. We test the valve before recommending ice maker module replacement, which avoids unnecessary cost.
My fridge compressor runs almost constantly — is that a sign of a problem? +
Yes. Continuous compressor operation usually indicates either a failed door gasket allowing warm air infiltration, a defrost system failure causing frost buildup that restricts cooling, or a refrigerant issue. We diagnose the specific cause — gasket replacement is often the quickest fix for this symptom in older Ranchlands fridges.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Ranchlands Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Ranchlands garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Ranchlands garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Ranchlands garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.

If your Ranchlands fridge starts behaving oddly in October or November as Chinook season begins, call us for a diagnostic visit before it becomes a full failure during the coldest months.
