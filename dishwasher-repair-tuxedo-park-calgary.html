Bosch dishwashers are increasingly common in renovated Tuxedo Park kitchens, and the most frequently encountered failure we service in this neighbourhood is the Bosch wash pump receiving an intermittent error due to a failing wash pump capacitor. The capacitor provides the startup boost for the pump motor — when it weakens, the pump struggles to reach full operating speed, causing poor wash performance and eventually triggering an E09 error code. Capacitor replacement is one of the more cost-effective Bosch repairs, typically completed in a single visit. Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water is also a factor on Bosch units: the triple-filter system (coarse, micro, and fine) requires monthly attention or calcium deposits from the hard water will gradually restrict water flow through the filtration stage, reducing wash pressure throughout the machine.

Samsung dishwashers installed during mid-2010s Tuxedo Park renovations frequently present with a specific drain hose check valve failure: the one-way valve that prevents drain water from flowing back into the sump degrades, allowing grey water to re-enter the machine at the end of the drain cycle, leaving a wet residue in the bottom of the tub. This condition also creates an unpleasant odour over time. Check valve replacement is straightforward, typically under $200 including parts, and restores proper drainage hygiene immediately.

My Bosch dishwasher shows E09 — what does that mean?+

E09 indicates a heating or circulation pump issue. In many cases it's caused by a weakened pump capacitor rather than pump motor failure — a significantly less expensive repair. Our technician tests the specific component on-site and provides a quote before proceeding.

Does Calgary hard water affect Bosch dishwashers in Tuxedo Park?+

Yes. At 150–200 ppm, Calgary's water requires monthly cleaning of Bosch's triple-filter system and periodic descaling of the spray arm nozzles. We perform a full descale during service and advise on maintenance to extend service intervals.
