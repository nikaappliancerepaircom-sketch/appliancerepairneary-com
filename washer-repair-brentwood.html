Common Washer Problems in Brentwood

  • Not spinning or agitating — motor coupling, lid switch, or drum bearing failure
  • Not draining — clogged pump filter, drain pump failure, or kinked hose
  • Leaking water — door boot seal (front-load) or tub seal (top-load) failure
  • Vibrating excessively — worn shock absorbers or drum spider arm damage
  • Error codes displayed — UE, OE, E1, F21 — we diagnose root cause, not just clear codes
  • Not starting — door latch, control board, or power supply issue

Hard Water & Washer Life in Brentwood

Washer Care Guide for Brentwood Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Brentwood homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Brentwood homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Brentwood homes.
