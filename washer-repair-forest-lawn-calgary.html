Common Washer Issues in Forest Lawn Homes

  • Not draining — standing water in drum — pump filter blockage or pump motor failure from heavy continuous use
  • Drum bearing noise (loud banging) — accelerated bearing wear in high-cycle LG and Samsung front-loaders
  • Not spinning or agitating — lid switch or drive belt failure in top-load units
  • Front-load door seal leak — boot seal deterioration from mineral deposits and continuous use
  • Washer stopping mid-cycle — thermal overload from continuous use or control board thermal fatigue
  • UE / SE error codes — drum imbalance or suspension failure in Samsung units running large loads

Calgary Hard Water & Your Forest Lawn Washer

In Forest Lawn's high-cycle rental households, Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water accelerates every component failure mode. Drain pump impellers develop calcium coatings that reduce pump output, drum seals accumulate mineral and biofilm buildup between cycles, and control board circuits in continuously-running machines experience elevated thermal stress. Our technicians advise Forest Lawn clients on two key maintenance practices: running a hot drum-clean cycle with descaling tablet every 4–6 weeks (not monthly, given high cycle counts), and using high-efficiency detergent dosed correctly for Calgary's water hardness — over-dosing creates additional residue that worsens mineral buildup.

Tip for Forest Lawn renters and landlords: In a household running six or more loads per day, the pump filter in a front-load washer should be checked and cleaned every 3 months — not the standard 6-month interval. A clogged pump filter is the leading cause of drain failures in high-use Forest Lawn washers and takes two minutes to clear when caught early.
