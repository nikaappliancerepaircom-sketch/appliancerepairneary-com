Kenmore Repair After Sears: Why Parts Availability Is Not a Problem

For decades, Kenmore was the flagship appliance brand sold exclusively through Sears. When Sears Canada closed its doors, many Toronto homeowners worried that servicing their Kenmore appliances would become impossible. The reality is quite different. Kenmore never manufactured its own appliances — they were built by Whirlpool, LG, Frigidaire, and other major manufacturers under the Kenmore label. That means nearly every component inside a Kenmore unit has a direct equivalent from the original manufacturer. Our technicians know exactly which cross-reference parts fit each Kenmore model number, and we keep the most commonly needed components stocked on our service vans.

Finding the right Kenmore parts requires deep knowledge of model number decoding. The first three digits of a Kenmore model number identify the actual manufacturer: 110 indicates Whirlpool, 795 means LG, 253 points to Frigidaire, and so on. Our team uses this system daily, allowing us to source the correct replacement part quickly and avoid unnecessary delays. Whether your Kenmore was built ten years ago or twenty, we can repair it.

Common Kenmore Repair Issues in Toronto Homes

After servicing thousands of Kenmore appliances across the GTA, we see certain problems more frequently than others. Kenmore refrigerators manufactured between 2008 and 2016, particularly the Elite French door models built by LG, often develop compressor failures that cause the fridge to stop cooling entirely. Our technicians carry the replacement compressor and the specialized tools needed for a sealed-system repair, so this job can typically be completed in a single visit.

Kenmore top-load washers are known for lid switch failures that prevent the machine from agitating or spinning. The plastic tab on the lid that activates the switch wears down over time, especially with heavy daily use common in larger Toronto households. We also frequently replace drive motor couplings on direct-drive Kenmore washers — a small plastic and rubber connector that absorbs shock between the motor and transmission. When it breaks, the washer fills with water but refuses to agitate.

Ice maker problems are another recurring theme in Kenmore side-by-side and French door refrigerators. Whether the ice maker has stopped producing altogether, is making undersized cubes, or is leaking water onto the shelf below, our technicians have diagnosed these exact scenarios hundreds of times and carry the replacement modules needed for a quick resolution.
