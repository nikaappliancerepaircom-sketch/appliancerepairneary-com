GE top-freezer refrigerators in Highwood develop defrost heater burnout as a common age-related failure. The defrost heater — a glass-enclosed resistive element mounted across the evaporator coil — burns out after years of operating under a scale jacket deposited by moist air passing over the coil. The coil then frosts over completely during normal operation. Cold air cannot circulate past the blocked evaporator, so the fresh food compartment warms progressively over 24 to 48 hours while the freezer remains cold. We replace the heater element and test the defrost thermostat and timer simultaneously, as all three components must function together for correct defrost cycling. Kenmore refrigerators in Highwood using the LG-platform linear compressor — sold under Kenmore branding in the 2010s — occasionally generate error codes indicating compressor communication loss. We diagnose whether the fault is the main control board or the compressor itself before making any recommendation, as compressor replacement cost must be weighed against the machine's age.

Samsung French-door refrigerators in renovated Highwood kitchens present ice maker problems consistent with our Calgary-wide experience. The ice maker fill tube ices over, blocking water from reaching the mould. The underlying cause is a combination of mineral scale on the inlet valve seat — accelerated by Calgary's 150-200 ppm water — and defrost cycle timing that allows the fill tube zone to fall below freezing during the off cycle. We clear the tube using a controlled thaw, service the inlet valve, and verify the defrost cycle parameters before closing the call. Secondary Samsung fridge calls from Highwood involve the ice maker wiring harness where it passes through the freezer door hinge — the harness flexes repeatedly with door opening and eventually develops a wire break that appears as an intermittent ice maker fault.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Highwood Calgary Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Highwood Calgary garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Highwood Calgary garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Highwood Calgary garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.

If your Highwood Calgary fridge starts behaving oddly in October or November as Chinook season begins, call us for a diagnostic visit before it becomes a full failure during the coldest months.
