Frequently Asked Questions — Rocky Ridge Dishwasher Repair

My Rocky Ridge Bosch dishwasher shows E15 — what does that mean?+
The Bosch E15 error means the anti-flood device has been activated — water has entered the base pan of the machine and the float switch has cut power to the pump. Common causes in Rocky Ridge Bosch dishwashers include a worn door gasket allowing water to drip into the base, or a pump housing crack. Do not run the machine until this is repaired, as continued operation can cause electrical damage. We carry Bosch door gaskets and can diagnose the source on the same-day visit.
Why does my Bosch dishwasher in Rocky Ridge leave a film on glasses?+
Almost certainly because the internal water softener is set at the factory default — calibrated for European water that is much softer than Calgary's Bow River supply at 150 to 200 ppm. When the softener is set too low, it does not remove enough hardness minerals, and these deposit on glasses and dishes as a white film. Recalibrating the softener to the correct setting for Calgary water resolves this immediately and permanently. We perform this adjustment on every Rocky Ridge Bosch service call.
Is it worth repairing a 12-year-old dishwasher in Rocky Ridge?+
Yes, for most single-component failures. Bosch, Samsung, LG, and Whirlpool dishwashers at 10 to 15 years still have years of service life if the repair addresses the failed component cleanly. Drain pump replacement, control board replacement, or door gasket replacement on a 12-year-old machine is a sensible investment if the rest of the machine is sound. Our $65 diagnostic gives you a clear repair-vs-replace recommendation.
