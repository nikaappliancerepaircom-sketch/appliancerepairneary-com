Freezer Repair Calgary Calgary's Trusted Specialists

Stand-alone freezers chest, upright, drawer, and the increasingly popular outdoor-rated garage freezers are a Calgary staple. The combination of bulk-buying culture, hunting and fishing households across the southern Alberta foothills, and large family meal-prep routines means almost every Calgary home with a basement or garage has a secondary freezer. That second freezer is also the one that fails most dramatically, because it lives in an uncontrolled-temperature space (garage, unfinished basement, or back porch) that takes the unit outside its design temperature window for half the year.

Our Calgary freezer technicians service every freezer configuration: chest freezers from Frigidaire, GE, and Whirlpool; upright freezers from Maytag, Kenmore, and Frigidaire; drawer-style freezer-only units from KitchenAid and Bosch; and the garage-rated models from Frigidaire and Whirlpool that are specifically designed to operate down to -18C ambient. We arrive with start relays, defrost timers and thermostats, evaporator fan motors, condenser fan motors, door gaskets, thermistors, and the diagnostic gauges needed for sealed-system work. About 74% of Calgary freezer calls finish on the first visit; the rest typically need a sealed-system component ordered.

Common issues

Common Freezer Problems We Fix in Calgary

Below are the freezer failures we see most often on Calgary service calls, with brief diagnostics for each. Our technicians arrive with the parts inventory required for these specific issues, so most calls are completed in a single visit.

Freezer not freezing temperature climbing
Loss of refrigerant charge (small leak, slow climb), failed evaporator fan motor (no air circulation), or compressor weakening with age. We pressure-test the system and verify amperage at the compressor.
Frost building up inside (manual-defrost or no-frost)
On no-frost models, defrost system failure (heater, thermostat, or timer). On manual-defrost chest freezers, this is normal but accelerated by frequent door opening or a worn gasket.
Compressor running constantly without ever cycling off
Refrigerant undercharge, poor airflow over the condenser coils, or a stuck thermostat that never satisfies the setpoint.
Loud humming or buzzing from the compressor area
Start relay or capacitor failing, compressor mounting feet degraded, or condenser fan blade hitting debris.
Garage freezer failing in winter (Calgary-specific)
Standard non-garage-rated freezers run their compressor based on the differential between cabinet temperature and ambient. When ambient drops below 10C in an unheated Calgary garage, the freezer thinks it does not need to run, and food in the cabinet warms up.
Door not sealing / gasket failing
Common in older chest freezers from Calgary basements, and accelerated by Calgary humidity swings. We replace the gasket and verify the door alignment.
Light staying on with door closed
Door switch failure minor issue but it warms the cabinet and runs up hydro costs. Easy 15-minute fix.
Water pooling at the bottom of an upright
Defrost drain clogged with food debris or ice. We clear the drain and verify the defrost cycle is running correctly.
Ice cream soft / not as cold as it should be
Either thermostat drift (sensor reading wrong), refrigerant slow leak, or condenser airflow restriction. We verify with an independent thermometer placed for two hours before deciding on parts.
Freezer making clicking sound but not running
Start relay failing unable to start the compressor. Common on older units (10+ years). Replacement is straightforward and inexpensive.

Calgary Garage Freezers: Why They Fail When You Need Them Most

The single most common Calgary-specific freezer failure we see has nothing to do with the freezer itself it has to do with where it lives. Putting a standard kitchen-rated freezer in a Calgary garage works fine for nine months of the year. From October through March, however, garage temperatures in Calgary regularly spend long stretches at or below freezing, and that is exactly when standard freezers fail.

Here is the mechanism: a freezer thermostat is calibrated to maintain cabinet temperature at roughly -18C (the standard food-safe setpoint), and it does this by comparing cabinet temperature to ambient. When ambient is +20C and cabinet is -18C, the thermostat correctly cycles the compressor to maintain the differential. But when garage ambient drops to -5C or colder common in Calgary winters the thermostat decides the cabinet is "cold enough" relative to the surroundings and stops calling for cooling. The compressor sits idle. Worse, the cabinet itself may slowly warm up because the unit is not running, and condensation can form on freezer interior walls when temperatures fluctuate around the freezing point. Frozen food gets ice crystals, freezer-burn rapidly, and meat may even partially thaw.

The fix is one of three options. First, buy a "garage-ready" freezer specifically rated for low ambient temperatures (Frigidaire, GE, and Whirlpool all make these the spec sheet will say "operates from -15C ambient" or similar). Second, retrofit a garage-kit thermostat onto an existing freezer (a heated thermostat add-on that tricks the unit into thinking ambient is warmer than it is). Third, move the freezer to a heated indoor space such as an insulated basement or utility room. We can advise during a service call which option fits your situation and your existing unit.

The second Calgary-specific freezer issue is dust. Calgary garages, in particular, accumulate fine dust from concrete floors, road salt residue, and dry winter air. That dust settles on the condenser coils at the back of the freezer (or underneath, on chest models), and within a year or two, coil heat rejection capacity drops 25-40%. The compressor compensates by running longer, hotter, and harder and eventually fails years before its expected service life. We routinely vacuum coils on Calgary garage freezers as a maintenance service.

Calgary freezer maintenance for long service life: First, vacuum the condenser coils every 6 months twice as often as the manufacturer recommends, because Calgary's environment is dustier than design assumptions. Second, check the door gasket every 12 months for cracks or hardening; replace it before it fails completely, since a leaky gasket forces the compressor to run constantly. Third, defrost manual-defrost chest freezers once a year minimum anything more than 1cm of frost adds insulation and reduces efficiency. Fourth, leave at least 5cm of clearance behind and above the unit for ventilation. Fifth, on garage units, verify the freezer is rated for low ambient operation; if not, plan to replace it or relocate it before next winter. Sixth, never store food directly against the cabinet walls the evaporator coils are usually behind the rear wall, and direct contact creates ice patches and inefficient cooling.
