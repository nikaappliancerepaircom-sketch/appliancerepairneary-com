Common Washer Problems in Charleswood

  • Frigidaire agitator dog clutch wear — the plastic dogs in the top-load agitator mechanism wear smooth over decades, causing the agitator to spin freely without engaging the clothes
  • Frigidaire motor coupler failure — the rubber coupler between motor and transmission degrades, causing a burning smell and loss of both agitation and spin
  • GE transmission seal leak — the lower tub seal around the transmission shaft deteriorates, causing a slow drip from the bottom of the machine during the spin cycle
  • Kenmore drum spider crack — the cast aluminium spider on older Kenmore front-loaders develops hairline cracks due to metal fatigue, causing excessive vibration and drum wobble
  • LG front-load drain pump bearing failure — the drain pump motor bearing seizes in LG machines more than 10 years old, causing a loud noise and incomplete draining
  • LG door latch interlock fault — the magnetic interlock in older LG front-loaders loses sensitivity, causing the machine to read the door as open mid-cycle and stop without an error code

Frequently Asked Questions

My old Frigidaire washer agitates but doesn't move the clothes — what's the problem? +
This is the classic symptom of worn agitator dog clutches. The small plastic dogs in the agitator hub wear smooth and the agitator spins without gripping the clothes. Replacing the clutch dogs takes about 30 minutes and restores full agitation for $80–$130.
Do you service Kenmore front-loaders with drum vibration problems? +
Yes. Vibration on Kenmore front-loaders is most often caused by a cracked drum spider (the aluminium arm that supports the drum from the rear shaft). We carry spider assemblies for common Kenmore front-load models and replace them in a single visit before the cracking progresses to drum bearing failure.
What does it mean when my LG washer stops mid-cycle with no error code? +
A mid-cycle stop without an error code on an LG front-loader usually indicates an intermittent door latch interlock fault. The machine detects an ambiguous door signal and halts as a precaution. The latch assembly replacement is straightforward and we test the door signal through a complete wash cycle before leaving.

Washer Care Guide for Charleswood Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Charleswood homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Charleswood homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Charleswood homes.
