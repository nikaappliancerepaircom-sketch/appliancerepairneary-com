Common Washer Issues in Inglewood Homes

  • Not spinning or agitating — drive belt, motor coupling, or lid switch failure in top-loaders
  • Loud banging or drum noise — worn drum bearings, common in front-load LG and Samsung units over 5 years
  • Not draining / standing water — blocked drain pump filter or failed pump motor
  • Door boot seal leak — front-load washer seal deterioration, accelerated by hard-water mineral deposits
  • UE / uE error codes — drum imbalance sensor or suspension rod failure in Samsung front-loaders
  • Musty odour from drum — biofilm and mineral buildup on door seal — we clean and advise on prevention

Calgary Hard Water & Your Inglewood Washer

Calgary's 150–200 ppm Bow River water supply creates ongoing maintenance demands for washers throughout Inglewood. Calcium deposits accumulate on drum seals, heating elements, and pump impellers — gradually reducing efficiency and accelerating component wear. Front-load washers are particularly susceptible: the door boot seal traps mineral-laden water after each cycle, promoting both seal deterioration and the musty odour that many Inglewood homeowners report. Our technicians clean drum seals, check and replace pump filters, and advise on washing machine cleaner cycles and detergent dosing calibrated for Calgary's water hardness — typically recommending a descaling cycle every 2–3 months for units in hard-water homes without softeners.

Local tip for Inglewood homeowners: If your front-load washer smells musty or the door seal shows dark staining, run a hot drum-clean cycle with a washing machine cleaner tablet monthly. In Calgary's 150–200 ppm water, skipping this maintenance allows mineral and biofilm buildup that eventually requires professional seal replacement — a preventable expense.
