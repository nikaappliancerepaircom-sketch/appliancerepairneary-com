Frigidaire top-freezer refrigerators in Greenview develop ice maker solenoid failures as a characteristic age-related fault. The solenoid — an electromagnetic valve that opens to admit water into the ice maker tray — develops a worn valve seat after years of calcium deposition from Calgary's 150-200 ppm hard water supply. The valve closes incompletely, causing a slow drip that either overflows the ice tray or drips down the freezer interior wall. We replace the solenoid valve and service the water inlet port to remove calcium scale before fitting the new part. GE refrigerators in Greenview frequently lose the damper control door — the plastic flap that regulates airflow between the freezer and fresh food compartment. A broken or seized damper causes the fresh food section to either over-cool or under-cool, depending on whether it's stuck open or closed. Replacement is straightforward and restores correct temperature distribution to both compartments.

Samsung refrigerators in renovated Greenview kitchens show ice maker problems consistent with our broader Calgary experience: the French-door ice maker assembly stops producing ice because the fill tube freezes. The root cause is a combination of Calgary hard water mineral scale on the inlet valve seat and a defrost cycle interval that is slightly too long for the freezer compartment temperature. We address both conditions simultaneously — valve service plus defrost interval check — to prevent immediate recurrence. GE defrost timers in older models occasionally fail in the run position, causing the defrost heater to stay on continuously and turn the freezer into a warming cabinet. Timer replacement is an inexpensive fix that is frequently overlooked when only the heater or thermostat is tested.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Greenview Calgary Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Greenview Calgary garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Greenview Calgary garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Greenview Calgary garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.
