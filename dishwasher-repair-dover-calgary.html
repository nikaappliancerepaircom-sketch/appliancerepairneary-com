Not Cleaning Dishes

Hard water calcification in spray arms and heating elements reduces wash effectiveness. Descale service and spray arm replacement restores full performance.

Won't Drain

Drain pump blockages and failed pump motors affect GE, Whirlpool, and Maytag dishwashers at age 15-25 years. Drain pumps replaced same-day.

Door Latch Failure

Broken door latches and worn door springs prevent the dishwasher from starting. We carry latch kits for GE, Whirlpool, and Maytag models.

Won't Fill with Water

Failed water inlet solenoid valves and stuck float switches prevent the tub from filling. Common in units over 15 years old.

Water Leaking

Door gasket deterioration and worn tub-to-pump seals cause leaks. We carry gasket kits for major brands and replace them on the first visit.

Not Drying Dishes

Heating element failures and blown thermal fuses leave dishes wet. Element and fuse replacement restores drying performance quickly.

My Whirlpool dishwasher won't drain — what's wrong?

Whirlpool dishwasher drain failures are most commonly caused by a failed drain pump motor, a blocked drain pump filter, or a kinked drain hose. We stock Whirlpool drain pump assemblies and typically resolve these issues in a single visit. The drain pump motor is usually the culprit in units over 12 years old.

Does hard water cause dishwasher problems in Dover?

Yes. Calgary's municipal water measures 150–200 ppm in calcium hardness, which is moderately hard. Over time, limescale builds up in spray arm nozzles, the heating element, and the wash pump, reducing cleaning efficiency and eventually causing mechanical failures. Using a quality rinse aid and running monthly descaling cycles significantly slows this process.

Is my GE dishwasher worth repairing if it's 20 years old?

GE dishwashers from the early 2000s are generally well-built, and most repairs — inlet valve, drain pump, door latch, heating element — cost $115–$220 and restore full function. If the control board has failed on a 20-year-old unit, we'll give you an honest comparison between repair cost and the cost of a new GE or comparable model before proceeding.
