Somerset's dishwasher population is distinctive in the way it reflects two different eras of technology side by side. The oldest units — GE and Frigidaire from mid-1990s builds — are mechanically simple machines where drain pump motor burnout, timer failure, and door gasket deterioration are the typical failure modes. The second generation — Samsung dishwashers installed during kitchen renovation waves in the 2000s and early 2010s — present with the Samsung 5E drain fault and 4E water supply errors that are now in their first major maintenance cycle. Both populations are worth repairing, but the diagnostic approach and repair strategy differ between them.

Calgary's hard water at 150–200 ppm has been a persistent factor in Somerset dishwashers regardless of brand or era. Calcium scale accumulates inside spray arm nozzles and on heating elements at a rate that is invisible to homeowners until dishes start coming out cloudy or spotted. On machines that are 20+ years old, the spray arm nozzles may be nearly completely blocked — we descale and clean these components during every service visit as a standard step rather than an optional add-on. Common Dishwasher Problems in Somerset Homes

  • Samsung 5E drain fault — blocked drain filter or pump impeller obstruction; cleared or pump replaced same-day
  • Samsung 4E water supply error — kinked fill hose or mineral-restricted water inlet valve; valve replaced
  • GE/Frigidaire drain pump failure — standing water after cycle in older mechanical-design dishwashers
  • Heavy calcium scale on spray arms — 20-30 years of 150–200 ppm Calgary water; full descaling included
  • Control board or timer failure — dishwasher won't start, gets stuck in a cycle, or runs continuously
  • Door gasket failure — water seeping onto floor from deteriorated door seal on older units

Repair Strategy for Somerset's Two-Generation Dishwasher Mix

For older GE and Frigidaire units (pre-2000), the typical failure modes are mechanical and the repair economics are straightforward: drain pump or heating element replacement at $130–$200 is clearly worth it if the tub and door frame are intact. For Samsung units from the 2000s-2010s, the 5E and 4E errors are frequently a blocked filter or restricted valve — often the cheapest repair category at $90–$150 — but can escalate to control board replacement at $220–$300 if the underlying cause is electronic. The diagnostic tells you which situation you are in, and the $65 fee is waived when repair proceeds. My Samsung dishwasher shows 4E — what does it mean?+

Samsung's 4E code indicates the dishwasher didn't receive enough water within the expected fill time. The most common causes are a kinked or restricted fill hose, a partially closed water shutoff valve, or a mineral-blocked water inlet valve. Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water is a frequent cause of inlet valve blockage in Somerset dishwashers. We test and replace the valve on the first visit.
