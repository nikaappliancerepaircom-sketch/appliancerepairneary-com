Fridge Repair in Signature Park — Aging GE and Whirlpool Units in Family Homes

Signature Park's single-family homes, built predominantly through the 1990s and early 2000s near the Westhills shopping district, house a generation of GE and Whirlpool side-by-side and top-freezer refrigerators that are now between 20 and 30 years old. These machines have been running continuously through Calgary's cold winters and warm summers, and at this age, defrost system components, evaporator fan motors, compressor start relays, and door gaskets are all prime candidates for failure. More recently, Samsung bottom-freezer and French door units purchased in the 2010s are now also entering their failure-prone decade.

The most common refrigerator fault in Signature Park homes is a gradual loss of cooling that begins with the fresh food compartment running slightly warm, then progressively worsens. In most cases this traces to defrost system failure — a burned-out defrost heater or a failed defrost thermostat allows frost to accumulate on the evaporator coil until airflow is blocked entirely. This is not a sealed system (compressor) failure, and it's one of the least expensive refrigerator repairs: defrost heater and thermostat replacement typically runs $130–$200 all-in for GE and Whirlpool units. We keep common defrost components in stock and can usually complete the repair in a single visit.

For Signature Park homes experiencing a complete loss of cooling — the fridge is warm and the compressor is not running — the fault is often a failed start relay on the compressor. This is a $20–$40 part that takes under an hour to replace, and it's far cheaper than compressor replacement or a new refrigerator. Our technician will confirm the diagnosis at the $65 diagnostic visit and give you a clear, no-obligation quote before any work begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

My fridge gradually stopped cooling over a few weeks — what's happening?+
Gradual cooling loss over weeks, starting with the fresh food section, is the classic pattern of defrost system failure. Frost builds up on the evaporator coil until airflow is blocked. The defrost heater, thermostat, or defrost control is the likely cause. It's not a compressor problem — repairs typically run $130–$200 all-in including labour.
My fridge makes a clicking sound and won't cool — can you fix it?+
A clicking sound combined with loss of cooling usually points to a failed compressor start relay — the small component that provides the initial electrical kick to start the compressor motor. It's one of the least expensive refrigerator repairs: the part costs $20–$40 and the total repair including labour typically runs $90–$150.
Do you service GE and Whirlpool refrigerators in Signature Park?+
Yes. GE and Whirlpool are the most common brands in Signature Park's older homes, and our technicians are trained on both platforms. We carry defrost components, start relays, and fan motors for GE and Whirlpool in stock and can typically complete the repair in a single visit.

Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Signature Park

Refrigerator repair calls in Signature Park peak in summer when compressors work harder, and in winter when Calgary's dramatic Chinook temperature swings — from −20°C to +15°C in 24 hours — stress door seals, defrost systems, and compressor components. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in Signature Park:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, evaporator fan, or thermostat failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in Signature Park.
  • →Ice building on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow. The fridge gradually warms up. More common in Signature Park during cold snaps.
  • →Water dispenser or ice maker not working — a failed water inlet valve or frozen fill line. Calgary's cold snaps can freeze water lines in poorly insulated spaces.
  • →Fridge running constantly — a worn door gasket lets cold air escape and forces the compressor to run non-stop. Calgary's Chinook cycles rapidly expand and contract rubber gaskets, accelerating deterioration.
  • →Clicking at startup or not starting — a failing start relay is a $60–$110 fix that prevents a full compressor replacement at $450–$750. Catching it early is critical.
