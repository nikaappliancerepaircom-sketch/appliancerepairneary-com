Common Fridge Problems in Charleswood

  • Frigidaire compressor start relay failure — the PTC start relay in older Frigidaire compressors fails, producing a clicking sound every few minutes as the compressor attempts and fails to start
  • Frigidaire defrost heater burnout — the tubular defrost heater element fails open after decades of thermal cycling, causing frost to accumulate on the evaporator and fresh-food temperature to rise
  • GE overload protector wear — the overload protector bimetal wears from years of trip events, eventually failing permanently open and leaving the compressor unable to start
  • Kenmore French-door ice maker solenoid — the water inlet valve solenoid on older Kenmore French-door fridges develops intermittent failures, causing ice maker to produce cubes erratically
  • Whirlpool evaporator fan motor blade crack — the plastic fan blade cracks and creates an imbalance that produces a rhythmic thumping sound every rotation
  • LG French-door sealed system leak — LG units from the mid-2000s to early 2010s are susceptible to sealed system refrigerant leaks that require evacuation and recharge

Frequently Asked Questions

My Frigidaire fridge makes a clicking noise every few minutes and isn't cooling — what is that? +
That clicking pattern is the compressor attempting to start and immediately tripping off — a classic symptom of a failed PTC start relay. The relay is a small inexpensive component that sits on the compressor and initiates the start cycle. Replacement takes 20–30 minutes and costs $85–$130. We carry PTC relays for common Frigidaire compressor platforms on the service van.
Is a 45-year-old Frigidaire refrigerator worth repairing? +
Very often yes. Original Frigidaire compressors from the late 1970s were designed to last 50+ years. The most common faults at this age — start relay, overload protector, and defrost heater — are inexpensive components. If the sealed system is intact, repair is almost always more economical than replacement. We assess the machine's overall condition honestly during the diagnostic.
My fridge has frost on the back wall of the freezer — does that mean the compressor is failing? +
Frost on the freezer back wall almost never indicates compressor failure. It's a defrost system problem — either the defrost heater has burned out or the defrost thermostat has failed open, allowing frost to accumulate without the heater clearing it. This is a common and affordable repair that restores normal operation.
Do you service LG sealed systems in Charleswood? +
Yes. LG sealed system refrigerant leaks are performed on-site including leak detection, evacuation, and recharge with the correct refrigerant type. This work is typically $260–$440 depending on the refrigerant quantity required.

Refrigerators and Calgary's Chinook Climate — What Charleswood Homeowners Need to Know

Calgary is famous for Chinook winds that can raise temperatures by 20–30°C in just a few hours — from −20°C in the morning to +10°C by afternoon. For refrigerators in Charleswood garages, unheated mudrooms, and basement utility spaces, these rapid temperature swings create a unique set of stress conditions our technicians deal with every winter.

  • Door gasket fatigue: Rubber gaskets expand and contract with each Chinook cycle. In Charleswood garages that swing between −20°C and +10°C multiple times per winter, gaskets deteriorate 2–3× faster than in climate-controlled spaces. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and can shorten its life by years.
  • Garage fridges stopping cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Charleswood garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off — the fridge thinks it's already cold — while the freezer warms up. A garage kit (available for select Whirlpool and GE models) corrects this.
  • Compressor start relay failures: Rapid temperature swings stress the start relay more than steady cold. A relay approaching failure will give out during the first extreme cold snap of a Calgary winter. Preemptive replacement ($60–$110) is far less disruptive than a January no-cooling emergency.

If your Charleswood fridge starts behaving oddly in October or November as Chinook season begins, call us for a diagnostic visit before it becomes a full failure during the coldest months.
