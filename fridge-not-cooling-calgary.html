Why your Calgary fridge has stopped cooling

A fridge that's not cooling is a time-sensitive problem — food spoilage begins within 4 hours at room temperature. Understanding the likely cause helps you decide how urgently to act. In Calgary, fridge cooling failures have a few patterns that differ from the national average, largely due to the city's temperature extremes and hard water supply.

Calgary's climate swings from −30°C winter nights to +35°C summer afternoons. Refrigerators in attached garages or against cold exterior walls in Calgary homes are stressed differently than fridges in climate-controlled interiors. We see a higher-than-average rate of garage refrigerator failures in Calgary during winter — most consumer fridges are not rated for ambient temperatures below 10°C, and the compressor can't run efficiently at sub-zero garage temps.

Most common reasons a fridge stops cooling in Calgary

Evaporator fan motor failure

The evaporator fan circulates cold air from the freezer compartment into the fridge section. When it fails, the freezer may still be cold while the fridge warms up — a classic symptom. Evaporator fan failure is among the most common fridge cooling issues we see in Calgary, particularly in Samsung and LG side-by-side models aged 5–8 years. Parts cost: $40–$85. Total with labour: $120–$210.

Defrost system failure — frost buildup

Modern frost-free refrigerators run an automatic defrost cycle every 8–12 hours. If the defrost heater, defrost thermostat, or defrost timer fails, frost accumulates on the evaporator coils until airflow is completely blocked. The fridge warms up gradually over 1–3 days. In Calgary's dry winter air, frost buildup accelerates when door seals are slightly degraded — cold, dry air infiltrates more readily. Manual defrost (unplug the fridge for 24–48 hours with towels underneath) will temporarily restore cooling, but the underlying defrost component failure must be addressed.

Dirty or blocked condenser coils

Condenser coils on the back or underside of the fridge release heat from the refrigeration cycle. In Calgary homes with regular dust accumulation or pet hair, coils can become coated within 1–2 years, dramatically reducing cooling efficiency. This is an easy, low-cost fix: coil cleaning with a vacuum and coil brush. If your fridge is running but not cooling as well as it used to, check the condenser coils first.

Compressor failure

The compressor is the heart of the refrigeration system. A failed compressor means no cooling at all — the fridge will be silent (no hum) or will start and stop rapidly (short-cycling). Compressor replacement is the most expensive fridge repair, typically $350–$600 in Calgary. For fridges over 10 years old, we honestly evaluate whether replacement makes more sense. For premium brands (Sub-Zero, Miele), compressor repair is almost always worth doing given the $5,000+ replacement cost.

Refrigerant leak

Refrigerant leaks are less common than mechanical failures but do occur, particularly in older fridges with copper tubing that has corroded. Symptoms: the compressor runs continuously, the fridge is slightly cool but never reaches target temperature, and you may notice a faint chemical smell. Refrigerant handling requires certified technicians under Canadian environmental regulations. We are certified for refrigerant recovery, evacuation, and recharge.

What to do right now if your Calgary fridge isn't cooling

  • Check the temperature setting — confirm it hasn't been accidentally changed (common in homes with children or after a power outage).
  • Check condenser coils — pull the fridge away from the wall and vacuum the coils if they're dusty.
  • Check door seals — close a piece of paper in the door; if it slides out easily, the seal is failing and warm air is infiltrating.
  • Move food to a neighbour's freezer or get ice if the fridge has been warm for more than 2 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my fridge stop cooling in Calgary?+
The most common causes in Calgary are: evaporator fan motor failure (freezer cold, fridge warm), defrost system failure causing frost-blocked airflow, dirty condenser coils, or compressor failure. Calgary's climate extremes can accelerate some of these failures, particularly in garage-located fridges during winter.
My freezer is still cold but the fridge section isn't — what's wrong?+
This pattern is almost always an evaporator fan motor failure or severe frost buildup on the evaporator coils (defrost system failure). The evaporator fan moves cold air from the freezer into the fridge section — when it fails, the cold stays trapped in the freezer compartment. Our technicians diagnose this in under 10 minutes.
How quickly can you fix a fridge not cooling in Calgary?+
Same-day service is available when you book before noon Monday through Saturday. Evaporator fan, defrost system, and condenser coil issues are resolved on the first visit. Compressor replacements may require a return visit if the specific compressor isn't in stock for your model.
Is it worth fixing a fridge in Calgary, or should I replace it?+
For fridges under 10 years old, repair is almost always more cost-effective than replacement. The exception is a compressor failure on a fridge over 12 years old, where $350–$600 in repair costs on an aging unit may not be justified. We give you an honest repair-vs-replace assessment at every diagnostic visit.
Can Calgary's cold winters damage my garage refrigerator?+
Yes — most consumer refrigerators are rated for ambient temperatures of 10°C to 43°C. In Calgary garages that drop below 0°C in winter, the fridge compressor may not run properly (it detects the ambient temperature as "cold enough") or the oil in the compressor thickens, causing damage over time. Look for a "garage-rated" refrigerator if you need reliable garage operation through Alberta winters.
