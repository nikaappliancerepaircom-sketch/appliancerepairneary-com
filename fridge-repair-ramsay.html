Calgary Hard Water & Your Ramsay Refrigerator

For Ramsay homes with refrigerators featuring water dispensers and ice makers, Calgary's 150–200 ppm Bow River water creates a predictable failure pattern. The water filter and inlet valve screen accumulate calcium over time, reducing water flow to the ice maker. The ice maker may still cycle — making the failure look like an electronics problem — but produce little or no ice because water pressure is insufficient. Our technicians always check water system flow rate on dispenser-related service calls, replacing calcified inlet valves and advising on the correct filter replacement interval for Calgary's water conditions: every 6 months in most Ramsay homes, not the standard 12 months recommended for softer water regions.

Local tip for Ramsay homeowners: If your fridge has been making a louder hum over the past few months, check whether the condenser coils at the back or base are dusty. In Ramsay's older homes with original hardwood floors, dust accumulation on condenser coils is a frequent finding — and cleaning them restores efficiency immediately without any part replacement.
