KitchenAid dishwashers installed in The Hamptons homes during the original 2000s construction are now in the 20-to-25-year service window — a period when the control board and wash motor components begin to show age-related failure. KitchenAid units of this generation develop a characteristic control board capacitor failure: the electrolytic capacitors on the main control board lose capacity over time, causing the board to fail to hold its programmed state and producing symptom patterns where the machine starts a cycle but fails to advance past the fill or wash stage, or displays cycle indicator lights that flash erratically. We replace the faulty capacitors or install a replacement board depending on the extent of damage. The high-mineral content of Calgary's water at 150-200 ppm accelerates internal scale formation in the wash pump impeller housing of KitchenAid machines, reducing flow rate and wash pressure progressively over years of operation.

Bosch dishwashers, popular in The Hamptons for their quiet operation in the open-plan kitchen designs of these larger homes, require particular attention to the water softener calibration issue common across all Calgary neighbourhoods. The Bosch integrated water softener's default hardness setting underestimates Calgary's actual 150-200 ppm hardness by a significant margin, causing premature scale accumulation on the heating element and spray arm nozzles even when homeowners maintain the salt reservoir correctly. The heating element scale layer acts as thermal insulation, causing the element to overheat and triggering the thermal cutout as a protective measure — the machine stops mid-cycle with a fault code indicating temperature failure. We recalibrate the softener to Calgary water hardness, descale the heating element if the scale layer is not yet causing permanent damage, or replace the element if the thermal cutout has been triggered multiple times.

In Hamptons homes with KitchenAid dishwashers from the early-to-mid 2000s, this pattern is typically caused by control board capacitor failure.
