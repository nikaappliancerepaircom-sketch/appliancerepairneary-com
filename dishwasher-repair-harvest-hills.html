The dishwasher fleet in Harvest Hills homes is predominantly Samsung, LG, and GE units installed during the 2000s and 2010s, now reaching the 15-to-25-year service window where first major failures occur.

Samsung dishwashers represent the highest volume of Harvest Hills service calls. Samsung units from the 2000s generation develop a specific failure in the diverter motor assembly — a motor that directs water alternately to the upper and lower spray arms during the wash cycle. When the diverter motor fails, the machine operates only one spray arm continuously rather than alternating, resulting in a wash cycle where one rack is cleaned adequately while the other receives minimal water contact. Homeowners typically notice that the upper rack is not being cleaned while the lower rack dishes emerge clean — or vice versa — depending on which diverter position the motor has failed in. The diverter motor assembly is accessed through the base of the tub and replaced as a complete unit. Calgary's 150-200 ppm water hardness deposits scale on the diverter valve seat over time, which can also cause the diverter to stick rather than rotate correctly, mimicking motor failure — we inspect the valve seat before recommending motor replacement. LG dishwashers in Harvest Hills of similar vintage develop a comparable issue in the circulation pump motor, where the pump's capacitor or start winding fails, causing the pump to hum without moving water.

GE dishwashers installed in Harvest Hills homes during the early 2000s show characteristic control board relay failure at this age. The relays that control the wash motor, drain pump, and heating element on older GE control boards develop pitted contacts after years of switching high current loads. Pitted relay contacts cause intermittent operation — the machine may run normally for several cycles and then fail to start the wash motor or the drain pump on the next cycle, producing inconsistent results that are initially mistaken for an electrical supply issue. We test relay contact resistance under load and replace individual relays or the full board depending on the extent of contact wear.

Dishwasher Repair in Harvest Hills — FAQs

My Samsung dishwasher cleans the bottom rack but the top rack dishes are always dirty. +
This is the hallmark of a diverter motor or diverter valve issue. The diverter alternates water flow between the upper and lower arms; when it fails or sticks, one arm operates while the other gets nothing. We inspect the valve seat for scale (from Calgary's 150-200 ppm water) and replace the motor if it has failed — restoring full upper and lower rack cleaning.
My GE dishwasher sometimes works fine and sometimes doesn't start the wash. +
Intermittent behavior in older GE dishwashers almost always traces to control board relay pitting. The relay contacts wear unevenly and make intermittent contact. We test the relays under load, replace the faulty ones, and the machine becomes reliable again. We act early to avoid the scenario where a completely failed relay shorts and damages other board components.
