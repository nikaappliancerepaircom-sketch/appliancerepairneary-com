Calgary's municipal water supply runs at 150–200 ppm dissolved calcium and magnesium. In northeast Calgary communities like Redstone, the water supply characteristics are the same as the rest of the city — a moderate hardness level that deposits mineral scale steadily across all water-contact surfaces inside a dishwasher. Without a home water softener or regular descaling maintenance, a dishwasher in Redstone accumulates scale in the circulation pump impeller, the spray arm bearing seats, the heating element surface, and the turbidity sensor lens over 5–8 years. The aggregate result is reduced wash pressure, poor drying performance, cloudy glassware, and eventually pump failure if scale accumulation is allowed to progress unchecked.

Samsung dishwashers in Redstone show the same control board relay failure pattern we see across Calgary's 2010s suburbs. These machines use a relay on the control board to switch power to the heating element and wash pump at different cycle stages. As the relay ages and its contacts accumulate micro-pitting, the machine develops intermittent faults: cycles that stop mid-wash, heating elements that fail to engage, or wash pumps that don't activate despite the cycle appearing to run. The key diagnostic challenge is that these relay faults can be intermittent — the machine may run normally for several cycles before failing again. We test under load to replicate the fault and confirm the relay as the cause before ordering a board replacement.

LG dishwashers in Redstone present with drain-related failures more frequently than their Samsung counterparts. LG's drain pump design in models from the early 2010s uses a compact impeller that is more sensitive to food debris bypass from the self-cleaning filter. When debris accumulates around the impeller, the pump works harder to overcome resistance and the motor winding temperature increases. Over time this causes winding insulation breakdown and eventual drain pump failure — manifesting as standing water at the end of cycles. We replace the drain pump assembly and demonstrate correct filter maintenance to Redstone homeowners to prevent recurrence, as the filter must be cleaned regularly to protect the pump in LG's design.

Common Dishwasher Problems in Redstone

Circulation Pump Scale from Hard Water

Calgary's 150–200 ppm hard water deposits scale in circulation pump impeller housings over 5–8 years. Wash pressure drops, spray arms slow, dishes emerge with white film or food residue. We descale the pump, clean spray arms, replace inlet valve screens, and recommend a rinse aid regimen to slow future buildup.

Samsung Control Board Relay Failure

Intermittent relay failure in Samsung control boards causes mid-cycle stops, heating element failures, or wash pump non-activation. Because the fault can be intermittent, we test under load conditions to replicate and confirm it before ordering a board — avoiding unnecessary replacements on working components.

LG Drain Pump Motor Winding Failure

Debris bypass around LG's drain pump impeller causes motor overheating and winding insulation breakdown. The result: standing water at cycle end. We replace the drain pump assembly and walk homeowners through correct filter maintenance to protect the new pump from premature failure.

Spray Arm Scale & Bearing Restriction

Upper and lower spray arm bearings accumulate mineral scale that progressively restricts rotation. The upper rack cleans poorly while the lower rack performs normally — a telltale sign of upper spray arm bearing restriction. We clean or replace bearings and flush supply towers in a targeted repair.

Door Latch & Interlock Failure

Door latches on 2010s-era Samsung and LG dishwashers develop mechanical wear with daily use. A latch that doesn't fully engage prevents the door interlock switch from activating and the cycle from starting. We replace latch assemblies and test the interlock circuit before completing the repair.

Heating Element & Drying Performance

Dishwashers that clean adequately but leave dishes wet often have heating element failures or thermostat faults that prevent the heated dry cycle from reaching proper temperature. We test element continuity and thermostat calibration and replace failed components to restore full drying performance.

Dishwasher Repair FAQ — Redstone

My LG dishwasher has standing water after every cycle. What's causing this?

Standing water at the end of cycles is almost always a drain system issue — either a blocked drain pump impeller, a kinked drain hose, or a failed drain pump motor. In LG dishwashers from the 2010s, the most common cause is debris accumulation around the drain pump impeller from insufficient filter maintenance. We clear the blockage and replace the pump if motor damage is confirmed, then walk through correct filter cleaning with you to prevent recurrence.

Why does my Samsung dishwasher stop mid-cycle sometimes but work other times?

Intermittent mid-cycle stops in Samsung dishwashers are characteristic of a control board relay fault. The relay contacts develop micro-pitting that causes intermittent failure under load. Because the fault isn't consistent, we test the machine under operating conditions to replicate the failure and confirm the relay as the cause before recommending a board replacement.

How often should I clean my dishwasher's filter in Calgary?

In Calgary's hard water environment, we recommend cleaning the dishwasher filter monthly and doing a vinegar descaling cycle every 3 months. For LG dishwashers specifically, the filter-to-pump clearance makes regular filter cleaning particularly important — debris that bypasses the filter can damage the drain pump over time.
