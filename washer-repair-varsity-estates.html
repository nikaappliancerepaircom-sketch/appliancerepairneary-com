My washer stops mid-cycle with no error code — what causes that? +
Mid-cycle stops without an error code are often caused by an intermittent door latch fault, a thermal fuse triggered by motor overheating, or a control board relay that loses contact under load. We test all three during the diagnostic visit.
Is a tub bearing replacement worth it on a 15-year-old washer? +
It depends on the machine. Premium brands like Miele and KitchenAid are designed to last 20+ years, so a tub bearing replacement at year 15 makes economic sense. We assess overall machine condition during the diagnostic and give you an honest cost-versus-replace recommendation.

Washer Care Guide for Varsity Estates Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. One that is not typically fails at 7–9 years. Calgary's hard water (150–200 ppm from the Bow River) and temperature cycling create extra wear — here is how Varsity Estates homeowners can extend their washer's life based on the repairs our technicians see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh or equivalent). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter from Calgary's hard water supply.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth in the drum. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration begin in Varsity Estates homes.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Calgary's Chinook temperature swings can stress hose connections — slow leaks at the connection point often go undetected until they cause floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Varsity Estates homes.
