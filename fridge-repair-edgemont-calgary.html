Calgary's hard water at 150 to 200 ppm has a direct impact on Edgemont refrigerators with ice makers and water dispensers. The water inlet valve — which supplies water to both the ice maker and dispenser — develops calcium scale on its internal solenoid screens and valve seats over years of use with Bow River hard water. Scale-restricted inlet valves cause the ice maker to produce undersized or hollow cubes initially, and eventually to stop production when flow restriction becomes severe enough. Frequently Asked Questions — Edgemont Fridge Repair

My Edgemont fridge is warm — how do I know if it's the compressor or just a relay?+
The quickest field test is to shake the start relay — a small component plugged into the compressor. If it rattles, the relay has failed internally and needs replacement. A dead relay makes the fridge appear as though the compressor has failed, but the fix is a $30 to $50 part rather than a compressor replacement. Our $65 diagnostic covers this test and distinguishes relay failure from compressor failure before you spend on either.
How does Calgary's hard water affect my Edgemont fridge's ice maker?+
Calgary's Bow River water at 150 to 200 ppm calcium carbonate deposits mineral scale on the water inlet valve solenoid screens and seats over time. This progressively restricts the water flow to the ice maker — you'll first notice smaller or hollow cubes, then reduced production, then complete stoppage. A new inlet valve restores full water flow. Using a refrigerator water filter rated for calcium reduction extends the valve's service life.
Is my LG Edgemont fridge compressor still under warranty?+
LG linear compressors carry a 10-year warranty in Canada. If your LG refrigerator was purchased between 2014 and 2020, the compressor may still be covered. We check the model and serial number during the diagnostic visit and advise on warranty status before recommending any compressor-related repair or replacement.
Can you repair my 30-year-old fridge in Edgemont?+
Yes, if the repair is economically viable. A 30-year-old GE or Maytag top-freezer in otherwise good condition with a failed start relay or fan motor is absolutely worth repairing. These old machines are energy-inefficient by modern standards but mechanically simple and often easier to repair than current models. If the compressor has failed on a 30-year unit, replacement is almost always the better financial choice, and we will tell you that directly during the diagnostic visit.
