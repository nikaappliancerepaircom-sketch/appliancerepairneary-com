Common Fridge Issues in Inglewood Homes

  • Not cooling or cooling inconsistently — faulty thermostat, compressor, or sealed system issue
  • Ice maker not producing ice — inlet valve blockage from mineral deposits, or ice maker assembly failure
  • Water dispenser slow or stopped — filter or inlet valve partially blocked by calcium at 150–200 ppm
  • Frost buildup in freezer — defrost heater, thermostat, or defrost timer failure
  • Loud compressor or fan noise — evaporator fan motor or condenser fan failure
  • Door seal not sealing — worn gasket causing energy waste and temperature fluctuation

Calgary Hard Water & Your Inglewood Refrigerator

Inglewood refrigerators with water dispensers and ice makers are particularly vulnerable to Calgary's 150–200 ppm water hardness. The water filter and inlet valve screen accumulate calcium deposits that restrict water flow and can cause the ice maker to cycle but produce no ice — a frustrating failure mode that's easy to misdiagnose as a compressor or electronics problem. Our technicians always check the water system flow rate during dispenser-related service calls and replace the inlet valve when calcification is detected. We also advise on filter replacement intervals for Calgary's water conditions — typically every 6 months rather than the manufacturer's standard 12-month recommendation for softer water regions.

Local tip for Inglewood homeowners: If your fridge is making a rattling noise that gets louder over time, the condenser coils may be blocked with dust and pet hair — especially common in Inglewood's older homes. Clean coils every 6–12 months by vacuuming the grille at the base. Dirty coils force the compressor to work harder, shortening its life by years.
