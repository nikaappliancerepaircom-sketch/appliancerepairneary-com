Calgary's persistent hard water has a compounding effect on dishwashers that have been running for two to three decades in Hawkwood homes. Pump impellers in older GE and Kenmore machines develop a calcium shell that restricts water flow and forces the motor to work harder — accelerating bearing failure and ultimately causing complete pump seizure. Spray arm nozzles in these units, originally drilled to precise tolerances, become partially blocked by scale deposits that redirect water flow and leave dishes cloudy or spotted. When we assess a 20 to 30 year old dishwasher in Hawkwood, we calculate whether descaling and pump service can extend the unit's life or whether the control board — often the next component to fail in aged machines — makes replacement the better economic choice. For dishwashers installed during kitchen renovations in the 2000s and 2010s, Samsung and LG units are the most common service calls: Samsung presents with 5C or OC drain error codes from pump filter blockage, while LG develops the OE drain fault and, in older direct-drive models, motor failure requiring a complete motor assembly replacement.

Bosch dishwashers have gained popularity in Hawkwood renovated kitchens, and these units require the internal water softener salt setting adjusted specifically for Calgary's 150 to 200 ppm range — the European factory default is calibrated for softer water and leaves dishes with a hard water film when left at stock settings. We recalibrate the Bosch softener on every Hawkwood service call. Whirlpool and KitchenAid dishwashers in the community frequently develop wash motor relay failure on the control board after 10 to 15 years; we carry Whirlpool W10805792 control board for same-day replacement. Our flat $65 diagnostic covers a full inspection of pump, spray arms, door latch, control board, and inlet valve — all the failure points common to Hawkwood's 1990s-to-2000s dishwasher stock.

Is it worth repairing a 25-year-old dishwasher in Hawkwood?+

Does hard water damage Hawkwood dishwashers faster?+
Yes. Calgary's Bow River water at 150 to 200 ppm calcium carbonate hardness deposits scale on every water-contact surface — pump impellers, spray arms, inlet valves, and heating elements. In a 25-year-old Hawkwood dishwasher, that represents two decades of scale accumulation. Using rinse aid consistently and running a monthly citric acid descale cycle meaningfully extends dishwasher life in the Calgary climate.
