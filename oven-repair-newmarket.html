Warning Signs — Call Appliance Repair Near Me Immediately

Some oven problems in Newmarket require immediate professional attention. Do not attempt to diagnose or repair these issues yourself — contact Appliance Repair Near Me at (437) 524-1053 right away if you notice any of the following:

  • Gas smell with the oven off — This is a potential gas leak. Do not flip any electrical switches. Leave the home immediately with the door open and call your gas provider first, then call us for oven diagnosis once the line is cleared.
  • Sparking or arcing inside the oven cavity — A damaged bake or broil element can arc against the oven wall, creating a fire risk. Turn off the oven at the breaker immediately.
  • Burning smell that is not food residue — Melting wire insulation or a failing control board can produce a distinct electrical burning odour. Power off the oven at the breaker and call for service.

  • Visible scorch marks on the oven element — A scorched element is about to fail completely and may arc. Replace it before the next use.

Maintenance Tips for Oven Owners in Newmarket

Regular maintenance extends the life of your oven and reduces the likelihood of emergency repair calls. Here are five practical tips for Newmarket homeowners based on the conditions and housing stock in your area:

  • Inspect the door gasket every six months — Press a piece of paper between the door and the oven frame, close the door, and pull. If the paper slides out easily, the gasket is not sealing properly and should be replaced. This is especially important in Newmarket homes where self-cleaning cycles are used regularly.
  • Clean the oven interior without abrasives — Use a baking soda and water paste instead of commercial oven cleaners with harsh chemicals. Abrasive cleaners can damage the oven cavity coating and expose bare metal to corrosion. Newmarket receives Toronto's treated Lake Ontario water supply, with the same moderate hardness that affects appliance longevity across the city, and mineral residue from steam cleaning can leave deposits that affect element performance.
  • Test the oven temperature annually — Place a standalone oven thermometer in the centre of the cavity and run a 350°F bake cycle for 20 minutes. If the reading is off by more than 25°F, the temperature sensor may be drifting and should be checked.
  • Run the self-clean cycle no more than twice per year — The extreme heat of a self-clean cycle (800-900°F) stresses the thermal fuse, door lock motor, and gasket. In Newmarket homes where ovens see heavy use, limiting self-clean frequency prevents premature component failure.
  • Protect against power surges — Install a surge protector on the oven circuit or a whole-home surge protector at the breaker panel. Summer thunderstorms in Newmarket cause voltage spikes that damage electronic control boards in Samsung, LG, and Bosch ovens.
